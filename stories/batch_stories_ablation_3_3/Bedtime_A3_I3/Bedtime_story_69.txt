In the year 2147, the lines between reality and dreams have faded within sprawling cities adorned with shimmering NeuroDorm Pods, where people connect through shared, immersive experiences. Elara Voss, a talented Dream Architect, passionately crafts dreamscapes that foster emotional connections, yet she wrestles with the implications of commodifying this sacred ritual. Standing on the frontline of societal change is Kai Riven, a vocal Advocate for Dream Rights, who organizes movements demanding equitable access to these technologies to bridge the widening gap between those who can dream freely and those left behind. Meanwhile, Juno Takashi, a Cultural Critic and Artist, highlights the complexities of collective dreaming, questioning the societal expectations that arise from shared experiences. As these three individuals navigate their worlds, they become entangled in a broader movement led by the Global Dream Councils, who strive to establish sleep as a fundamental human right, setting the stage for a collective awakening through the nightly escape into dreams.

Tensions rise between corporate representatives and equity advocates as dialogues deepen, exposing disparities in resources and experiences.
