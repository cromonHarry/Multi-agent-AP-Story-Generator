In the year 2145, urban landscapes buzz with drones delivering dental supplies, while holographic advertisements tout the latest in DIY dental tech for the affluent. Amidst this chaos, Juno Tanaka wanders the streets of Neon City, a place once thriving with his dental practice now transformed into a shadow of its former glory. As he observes the stark divide between gleaming clinics and the underground co-ops of the marginalized, he recalls why he became an advocate. Nearby, Ava Choi orchestrates an augmented reality performance, intertwining art with activism, as Kellan Carlsson remarks on his bio-sliding scale idea, pondering its impact. Meanwhile, Marisol Reyes passionately trains community members to harness their biowearables, aiming to empower them in a world where smiles come with price tags. Together, these characters embody the struggle between corporate interests and a burgeoning movement for dental equity, setting the stage for a revolutionary battle for a basic human right.

As tensions escalate, a public protest orchestrated by Juno and Ava attracts a crowd in front of a high-end dental tech exhibition, demanding equal access to dental care. The event draws media attention, igniting a wave of solidarity among marginalized communities. However, Kellan's company faces backlash from corporations whose profits are threatened, leading to aggressive attempts to discredit the movement. Meanwhile, Marisol's grassroots training sessions encounter sabotage from rival factions that aim to undermine DIY dentistry. Amidst this chaos, the newly coined 'smile currency' emerges as a rallying point for the community, solidifying the movement's resolve to fight against entrenched inequities.

In a climactic confrontation at the glitzy unveiling of Kellan's revised bio-sliding scale, where access to dental care faces its ultimate test, Juno leads a passionate coalition of advocates and community members. Armed with augmented reality projections crafted by Ava, showcasing the stark realities of dental inequities, they storm the venue, voicing their demand for universal access. As the media captures the moment, Marisol's trained grassroots members distribute 'smile currency' to attendees, sparking chaos. Kellan, torn between profit and conscience, witnesses the grassroots movement's sheer force, leading him to finally denounce the corporate facade, catalyzing a seismic shift in both industry and society.

In the aftermath of the tumultuous confrontation, Juno, Ava, Kellan, and Marisol unite to forge a new coalition, inviting both community members and corporate representatives into a dialogue about dental equity. The underground co-ops experience an influx of support, as the media amplifies their message of universal access, urging the affluent to rethink their values. Kellan pledges to redirect his startup towards affordable dental solutions and collaborates with Marisol to create accessible training programs. As the community celebrates small victories, the streets of Neon City begin to transform, with the once-divided populace coming together under a shared commitment to equitable health, signaling a burgeoning revolution in dental care.
