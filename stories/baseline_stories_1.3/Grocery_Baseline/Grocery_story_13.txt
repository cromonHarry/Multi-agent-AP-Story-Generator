In the year 2045, grocery shopping has transformed drastically. Traditional supermarkets have been replaced by sprawling virtual marketplaces controlled by mega-corporations. Customers use augmented reality (AR) devices to project virtual items into their homes, where AI-driven personal chefs interactively prepare meals based on nutritional needs and preferences. Hailey, a tech-savvy food critic, Jo, her data-obsessed partner who manages an AI grocery start-up, the elderly butcher Mr. Rourke who struggles to hold onto his knife skills in a world dominated by automation, and Willow, a savvy journalist exposing the dark secrets behind mega-grocers, converge at one of the few remaining autonomous local markets in what used to be a vibrant neighborhood. Recognizing the implications of losing the few markets left that provide real food—and not merely manufactured proteins—each character has pressing reasons to ensure this small shop survives amid corporate giants.

As food shortages become an increasing concern due to fluctuating climate patterns and corporate monopolies, the characters become aware of a clandestine plan by MegaGro Cerulean Supply. Their ultra-convenient subscription-service model, while appearing beneficial, is actually designed to drive traditional markets out of business, leaving consumers completely reliant on synthetic alternatives. Hailey, always on the lookout for stories with a bite, uncovers this plan while investigating the Silicon Valley-based company that provides a new line of organic protein substitutes that use nanobots to optimize nutrients. Unable to stay silent—both for the sake of her profession and because of her alarm over increasing dependency on synthetic food—she gathers her friends to plot a response. Together, they unveil the underlying agendas entangled in greed and big tech that fuel HyperGro Synthesis, which supplies most urban areas.

Determined to fight back at what they see as an ongoing assault on food culture, the eclectic group plunges into a multi-faceted protest. They plan a dining experience featuring traditional butchered meats prepared by Mr. Rourke, contrasting it with the masses of hyper-packaged “food-like” substances popularized by MegaGro. They ensure a panel conversation takes place during the event, pulling real-time data showcasing the negative impacts of synthetic foods through Jo’s vast technologies. Driving sentiment against commercial food cultures, they assemble like-minded local businesses and community members.
