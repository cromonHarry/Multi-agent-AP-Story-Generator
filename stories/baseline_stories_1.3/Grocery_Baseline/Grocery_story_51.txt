In the year 2045, grocery shopping has transformed into an immersive virtual experience. People no longer stroll through aisles or weigh fruits. Instead, they enter a digital market powered by a VR infrastructure known as The Cart, which simulates a sprawling grocery store infused with personal tastes and sustainability ratings. This innovation is spearheaded by tech mogul Layla Kaine, whose dedication to sustainable sourcing took center stage after an unprecedented environmental disaster made traditional farming nearly extinct. Accompanied by her skeptical partner and chief engineer, Marco Yang, and digital anthropologist Rhea Wess, a former grocery clerk, they work on expanding The Cart’s artificial intelligence to be more in tune with human habits. The story starts with Layla preparing for a live demonstration of the next phase of development intended to make grocery shopping a seamlessly interactive social experience for an pumped digital influencer, Clara Alvear.

As the team gears up for the upcoming PR event, complications begin to arise. Users begin to notice discrepancies between their curated grocery lists and the discrepancies that arise during live synchronization—a phenomenon that confounds Marco since such glitches should not happen.
