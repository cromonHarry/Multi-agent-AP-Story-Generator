In a world where Earth has become a sprawling metropolis sustained by advanced geo-gardens and vertical farming, the act of grocery shopping has transformed into an intricate ritual. Customers no longer walk aisles of traditional supermarkets; instead, they visit Biocube Markets—self-contained agricultural ecosystems where exotic fruits, nutrient-packed vegetables, and lab-grown meats flourish under customizable holographic biomes. Mainstream technology like holo-connect tablets lets shoppers experience grocery shopping through augmented reality, featuring lifelike representation of food before making their selections. The semi-artificial Stefan owns a popular Biocube in Neo-San Francisco and has a knack for mixing horticulture with algorithms. Alongside him are Lila, his tech-savvy partner who was a botanist before the Biocube boom; Niyah, a passionate trend journalist exploring consumerism, and Jude, an activist fighting against the unchecked evolution of this new agriculture industry.

As modern grocery shopping eliminates the stress of mundane tasks, it raises deeper questions about consumer culture and sustainability. Niyah, searching for stories worth telling, laments how these eco-pyramids shine under slick advertisements, downplaying the environmental threats behind their production. Meanwhile, Lila starts experiencing unsettling visions during her nightly dream cycles about a lush green landscape overrun by synthetic produce, leading her to investigate connections between herbicides used in Biocubes and ecological systems harmed. Jude, on a mission, approaches Lila and Stefan to persuade them to drag the realities behind the façades back into the spotlight, yet he faces resistance since exposing such glaring contradictions might jeopardize biotechnological advancements that boast to secure food scarcity.
