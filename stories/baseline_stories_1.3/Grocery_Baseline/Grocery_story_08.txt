In the year 2042, societal structures have pivoted around automated living, reducing human interaction in daily activities. In this transformed world, grocery shopping has evolved into a holographic experience where individuals can ‘enter’ virtual superstores from the comfort of their homes. Key to society’s subtle hierarchy is the national grocery app, HoloMart, which utilizes vast algorithms and personal behavior analytics to suggest deveined meal packages suited to just about anyone’s tastes and health needs. Amelia, a food historian hesitant to embrace technology; Leon, a pizza craftsman who attaches emotional significance to food; Sofía, a tech-savvy designer passionate about sustainable food; and Sam, a bold marketing strategist manipulating social media trends play crucial roles as the nation grapples with the exploration of self-defined food values and ethics.

One day, Amelia stumbles upon a tale of a grocery past filled with creativity and human interaction that no longer exists. Inspired, she recruits Leon, Sofía, and a hesitant Sam; together, they hatch a plan to rekindle authentic relationships with groceries and generate joy around their preparation and consumption. As Rachel, competitor company's AI leader passionately debates HoloMart’s limitless abundance with them, the firmly automated world they live in is highlighted as their individual disenchantment surfaces. Struggling against sleek marketing, colorful glamour campaigns, and unattainable personalization aims distorting true passions in food, they set out to obliterate HoloMart’s content constraints through curating unexpected food gatherings in public spaces featuring nostalgic treats blindly reminisced.

As their grassroots campaigns begin to flourish, mini pop-up themed grocery sharing events illuminate subdued sparks of rebellion in the local communities.
