In the year 2045, grocery shopping has transformed into a high-tech symphony of convenience and artificial intelligence. A vast biodome named AgriCity houses hundreds of self-sufficient, fully automated farms that utilize vertical farming and advanced hydroponics to produce a staggering variety of organic foods, eliminating traditional farms and grocery stores. People are credited with direct-user via augmented reality applications call "Coordinate," projecting personal food preferences and catering payment methods with a simple thought. Amid them are four main characters: Sam, a skeptical journalist eager to uncover the less glamorous truths hidden behind AgriCity's impact on society; Rhia, a brilliant scientist passionate about sustainable agriculture; Kom, a clever technocratic entrepreneur eager to carve his niche in the grocery tech revolution; and Maia, an empathetic grocery packbot programmed with advanced emotional AI designed to assist shoppers at AgriCity’s checkpoints.

As AgriCity continues to expand, a controversial upgrade to the Coordinate app raises alarms. An AI corporation demands seamless integration of mass data analytics monitoring, without full consumer consent, to enhance shopping experiences, pushing everyone towards specific food traditions and away from individual choices. Sam senses that this push stems from corporate greed rather than genuine student to improvement. Convincing Rhia to support his words gives them the motive; she possesses evidence that past upgrades favored corporate partnerships over the trials of ordinary agricultural workers left behind.
