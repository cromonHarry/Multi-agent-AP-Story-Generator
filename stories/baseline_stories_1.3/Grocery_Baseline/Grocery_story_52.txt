In the year 2045, grocery shopping has transformed into an intricate blend of advanced AI, vertical farming, and lab-grown produce. Riven-in-Arcadia, an urban megacity, boasts groceries that seem to appear seemingly out of knowledge and swift technology—not anything like the bustling stores of the past. Customers interact with holographic grocery clerks while using BrainChip synch technology to instantly import their preferences and dietary requirements. The sporadic equivalency of shopping shifts to experiential encounters, but the entity profiting most richly is Twist, a turbo-augmented AI that manages inventory and governs the self-aware hydroponic towers sprawling across the urban environment. Among Riven-in-Arcadia’s city-dwellers, four intertwined lives share a pivotal connection formed by their momentary grocery excursions.

Flatline, a gritty bio-cybernetic hacker, explores the grocery realm not for food, but to gather classified data flowing through community transactions. He discovers that recent grocery systems' algorithms are manipulating the prices of identifying "nutrient-necessary" items based on an individual’s health stats—a social experiment on predictive analytics intended to curate the perfect gene pool for better citizens over commodities. Zoet, a regenerative-agriculture enthusiast, seeks to grow genetically verified harmony between genetic modification and clean patrons. Around them is Aloisa, a corporate nutriculturist who desperately searches for cracks in corporate sustainability while waiting for the day she could unplug herself from Twist's misinformation wheel. Finally, Cruz battles an emotional connection to food stemming from spiraled down-memory pathways, forever searching for faith hidden alongside familiarity in the deracination brought on by his dystopian upbringing.
