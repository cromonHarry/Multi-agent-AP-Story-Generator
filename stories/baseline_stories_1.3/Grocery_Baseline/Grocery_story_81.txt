In the year 2045, the concept of grocery shopping has been revolutionized by advanced automation and biotechnology. Supermarkets are replaced by Grocery Pods, autonomous, modular vending stations that utilize AI to analyze customer preferences, nutritional needs, and supply chain data. Each pod, dispersed strategically throughout urban landscapes, can customize food items to personal DNA profiles, ensuring offerings are tailored to enhance wellness and digestion. Buyers belong to a Network, an online community that allows them to ship localized flavors directly to their pods, supporting small growers over mass-produced supermarket chains. Amid this landscape, four characters struggle with their relationship to food and each other: Maia, an outreach worker developing sustainable eating habits; overdemanding entrepreneur Brooke, who relies heavily on the convenience of fish-print protein cubes and algae-based snacks; former chef Leo, struggling to reskill after being displaced by automations; and Elijah, a coder building algorithms for predicting food trends.

As Maia advocates for nutritional awareness and local gardening initiatives, she finds resistance. Consumers blinded by convenience flock to the automatic Grocery Pods and their synthetic delicacies, buying into the widespread belief that health is only a digital beat or "one tap away." All while, Leo covertly develops what he openly calls ‘Augmentation Cuisine,’ aiming to restore flavor and authenticity lost in precision production. At the same time, Elijah’s successful algorithm increasingly pushes for rarer, indulgent foods that spark trends but dismiss genuine sustenance. A plant converts exclusive altitude foliage to popular aliases and flavors based solely on revenue tracks, working against Donovan advocacy and ethos. All four meet weekly for Chip Dinner at the local Vegan Palace, navigating their conflicting dialogues about health and flavors when an experimental protein sourced unexpectedly results in mild food poisoning in one consumer. The news hits just as businesses in the Network rally around market reform and anti-Grocery reforms, with cries deemed PRIME-casualomie, spreading easily and swiftly throughout the digital realm.

Caught in a tangled web of events, the characters converge at their next Dinner where fear intensifies when the emergency orders fail to comply, fancy trends imitate innovation, transforming snacks in real-time.
