In the year 2045, society has adapted to a time when food scarcity reached critical levels and technological solutions integrated deeply into daily life. In a small urban enclave, the Grocery Nexus stands as a massive automat, selling not just physical food items but experiences and sustainable products, all curated through AI algorithms. Ava, an entrepreneur and herbalist, seeks to share ancient knowledge of nutrition gleaned from Grandma’s hand-me-downs, blending with new dietary trends. Aiden, an algorithm engineer who works for Nexus, believes in the system, despite the eerie predictability and societal dependency his job has exacerbated. Economically distant yet romantically linked, their differing philosophies create tension; meanwhile, Finn, the smug marketing head of Nexus, always delights in leveraging glimpses of its world history to enthrall customers, driven by profit and click ratings over genuine sustenance. Finally, Zara, a groundbreaking food engineer, backs ethical tech and promotes new 3D-printed food fabrics, echoing both liberation and fear of reliving apocalyptic deficiency.

The malfunction of the Grocery Nexus crowds one rainy evening when a cadre of patrons stretching a full block form an unruly queue waiting desperately for dinner options, exposing an overdependence on the system they all relied upon. With no perishable items delivered after critiques mounted against excessive chemical preservatives, consumers face chaos at the doors. Inspired by this unusual riot, Aiden approaches Ava with an idea to initiate a culinary renaissance by launching a rebellion against synthetic food, speculating that they could leverage the tech-controlled popularity of gourmet ingredients using grandmother's wisdom that emphasizes quality and nutrition.
