In the year 2045, society relies entirely on HoloGroceries—a revolutionary advancement where physical food is reduced to adaptive holograms that project flavors and qualities at the push of a button. Modeled for efficiency, the industry streamlines food production by centralizing cultivation indoors under holographic emissions, ensuring that physical loss is virtually eliminated. Among ubiquitous virtual grocery outlets, Amelia, a sustainability activist frustrated with this new food paradigm, decides to gather a group of likeminded individuals, including James, a data analyst for a grocery conglomerate, Sage, an underground chef who champions culinary craft, and Theo, a bounty hunter disillusioned with society. Their private coalition seeks to understand the impact of this tech on health, ecology, and culture, resulting in clandestine attempts to expose the darker secrets of the HoloGrocery industry.

As Amelia and her friends decode the intricate system supporting HoloGroceries, they discover that beneath the glossy digital structure lies a murky realm of monopolistic control and inequality. Large corporate families inadvertently worsen environmental crisis with lesser-known synthetic pesticides saturating their holographic feeds emitting carcinogen-like effects—as indicated by anonymous reports generated by James’s AI algorithms. This revelation prompts a desire among the group to investigate further and embarrass high-ranking executives influencing the system for profit, particularly a shadowy figure named Victor Maxwell known for sub-contracting corners from a duplicitous mural crammed along urban borders. Venturing into an elusive downtown office renowned for its adjacency to downtown vendors, confronting security drones that will extinguish any surge to truth along with pragmatism, the friends devise a foolhardy CI strategy that explicitly mimics ordered HoloGroceries.

The climax strikes as the group slyly accesses Victor's system.
