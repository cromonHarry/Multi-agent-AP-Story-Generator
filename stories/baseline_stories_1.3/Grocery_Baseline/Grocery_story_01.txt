In 2045, grocery shopping has undergone a radical transformation, driven by advancements in technology and farming techniques. Vertical farms soar above cityscapes, using artificial intelligence to optimize crop yield. Delivery systems fully automated, use drones to provide immediate and fresh produce directly to consumers’ homes. Lexi, a head agricultural scientist, believes deeply in enhancing nutrition and decreasing waste, hoping to launch her new bio-engineered produce at the forefront of the industry. Meanwhile, her husband Iris is a data ethicist advocating for transparency in food sourcing, raising concerns over the hidden implications of genetically-modified goods. Complicating matters is Tom, Lexi's ambitious colleague who seeks wealth through aggressive commercialization of their innovations, straddling the line between ethical responsibility and corporate greed. Finally, Sarah is an independent journalist suspicious of the booming grocery innovations, determined to unveil any murky truths hiding beneath the surface.

As the launch of Lexi’s novel fruits approaches, tension escalates at the grocery mega-warehouse dominated by corporate interests. After collecting mysterious public feedback showcasing aversion to the new modifications, the team holds a fiery meeting where Lexi argues for the reconfiguration of their product line towards sustainability rather than haste in profit. Against this backdrop of discord, Iris stumbles upon unethical data manipulation in their consumer profiling system. Sensing imminent dangers related to both the misleading food cultivation processes and hush-hush agendas from the agro-political stakeholders, his conscience urges him to collaborate with Sarah to expose these truths, as she is also wary of the increasing transparency perplexities alongside societal implications.

At the height of the emotional fervor, catastrophe strikes during a highly-publicized event meant to demonstrate the delicious and exotic tastes of their new line. A technical failure results in an uncontrollable cannabis-related contagion sown inadvertently among the genetically modified crops, leading to uproar within the community as people fall ill and panic spreads healthier caution over new-age foods. Lexi valiantly works to neutralize the risks emerging, driven not only by her ambition to pull success from disastrous circumstances but also by undeniable chaos to which society is slipping. In these chaotic stirrings is where relationships fray: with Tom advocating concealment of liability and maintaining monetary success while neglecting human cost and Sarah argues for powerful believers to terminate production until everything stabilizes. 

Striving through rubble flecked with injured pride and tainted commitments, doors slam finality in corporate gardens.
