In the year 2040, the traditional practice of dentistry has undergone a radical transformation, replaced by a sophisticated system known as "Oral Tech Clinics." These facilities leverage advanced biotechnologies such as nanobots and genetic reprogramming to maintain dental health with minimal human intervention. Patients can sign into their Oral Tech via a neuro-linked interface seated comfortably at home, receiving real-time check-ups while the clinics analyze their conditions. Clive, an anxious patient reluctant to embrace technology, finds himself facing the imminent extraction of all his teeth one fateful day. Meanwhile, Dr. Elena Wu, a brilliant but disillusioned dentist-turned-technocrat, heads one of the most advanced Oral Tech Clinics. Lacey, a fast-talking marketing executive with a sharp tongue and an eye for innovation, seeks ways to make the new practice seem less intimidating. At the same time, Roger, Clive’s no-nonsense best friend, senses his lingering fears about becoming devoid of any actual teeth. 

Troubled thoughts haunt Clive—in a society dependent on technology, he feels powerless and anxious. With a scan scheduled this evening, he ruminates on what it would mean to relinquish the last physical aspects of his humanity. At the clinic, while intensity grows with new patients arriving and technologically assisted procedures buzzing around, Dr. Wu watches desperately as the heart and soul of traditional dentistry fades from her role. Fueled by her artistic desires and childhood dreams, she knows her clinic has become a machine, Walt Whitman-inspired verses rattling in her mind trapped between freedness and enforced digitalization. Lacey sits by Dr. Wu, pitching innovative curing and interactive methods for advertising, including augmented reality graphics that would diminish Clive’s trepidations altogether. Roger unwittingly sparks idea transformations as he delivers his friend to the clinic while coming to terms with his own resolve of fear and technological defiance.
