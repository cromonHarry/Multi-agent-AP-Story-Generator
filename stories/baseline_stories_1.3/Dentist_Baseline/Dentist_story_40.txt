In the near future, dental care has transcended its traditional confines thanks to advancements in nanotechnology and artificial intelligence. People like Dr. Mira Agee, a celebrated dentist and revolutionary bio-engineer, have harnessed these innovations to render dental procedures painless and quick. Her clinic, BioDent 2.0, claims no one should ever fear a dentist again. However, a clandestine group, the Dentists of the Past—mostly old-school practitioners and skeptical patients—believe that technology should not overshadow the personal touch of human care. They argue that the transformation of the dental profession implies a social disconnection, undermining the depth of interpersonal relationships that once defined patient care. Bob, Mira’s childhood friend, is a marketing analyst whose loyalty lies with her yet struggles with the ethical ramifications of outsourcing human agency to machines in a field that should prioritize compassionate care.
