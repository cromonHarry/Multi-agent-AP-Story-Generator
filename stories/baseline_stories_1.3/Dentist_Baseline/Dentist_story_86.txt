In the year 2045, dental health has transformed dramatically with the advent of Gerostim drones, artificial intelligence bio-integrators, and a cultural shift toward preventive health magic. Consumers now regain pristine, sparkling conditions of their teeth without setting foot in a clinical environment; instead, patients upload their dental DNA to the cloud and cease all need for human intervention in their care. At the forefront of this system is Dr. Maelin Torres, not merely a dentist but a dental architect who designs personalized care programs enhanced by non-invasive regimens. Dr. Torres's trusted aide, Anya Ko, serves as a charming interface for patients, adding warmth to the cold orchestration of technology, whereas Gregory Fenn, a synergetics researcher, questions the ethical implications lurking beneath this pristine veneer. At the other end of the spectrum is Ian Reyes, a rebellious artist resistant to the mainstream reliance on dental technologies—his propagation of anti-dental murals critiques the technological siege on natural healing.

During one of the holographic consultative sessions, a transmission error muddles the network communication, leading to a frightening revelation; Ian, against trusting artificial intelligence for his oral health, finds himself locked into a battle for control over the system when he accidentally convinces his peers that the artificially designed dental practices are fracturing their natural connections. This escalates as Gregory joins Ian, stoking passions against what they deem a surrender to mechanized physiology. Meanwhile, Dr. Torres heavily braces herself against this revolt; fear of the backlash warrants action, and Anya quickly mobilizes doctors from around the globe using a disarmingly captivating public relations campaign invoking ancestral "Healthy Remembering" where witnesses recount generational tales of empowered oral health without tech.
