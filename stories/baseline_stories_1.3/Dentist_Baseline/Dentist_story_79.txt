In the year 2042, dentistry has transformed from a manual profession into a sophisticated field of biotechnology. A video file that buzzed around social media showed robotic wizards called "NanoDent" sweeping through oral healthcare at unprecedented speeds. Instead of traditional drills and chisels, lasers and nanobots replace unformed gum tissues and cavities. Marla Kinsey works as a leading dental engineer, who designs and programs the NanoDent systems. Alongside her is Dr. Felix Rhodium, a former dentist turnedptunewhat.com—providerrobot, hoping to embrace advancements in technology while losing the burdens of manual labor. The story follows them and their assistant, Rasaan, a sentient AI focused entirely on patient experience. Tensions bubble in the company's main facility when Otto Vinegar, a struggling dentist advocating for the significant human interaction aspect of dentistry, threatens to turn medical boards and public opinion against their innovations, warning that extensive automation could lead to dangerous dehumanization in patient care.

With their clinic’s demonstration day approaching, tensions escalate as opinions within and outside their team clash about the extent of technology's role in health care. Marla tirelessly programs enhancements designed to read a patient’s emotional signals while Chrome Skimming suits mold the NanoDent's procedures. Rasaan tries to maintain peace, bantering and chatting with patients, eager to offer the charming human touch among the arrays of machinery that typically dominates their sector of care. Meanwhile, Otto meticulously gathers testimonies from disillusioned clients experiencing paranoia since switching to NanoDent. He envisions recruiting a brigade of former dentists to reclaim patient trust away from the automated systems. It reaches a boiling point when Rasaan catches wind of some altered testimonies about betrayal related to robotics by anesthetized patients.

The dazzling day has arrived, and crowds gather to see NanoDent’s world premiere as it promises to redefine patient experiences in preparing for and undergoing dental procedures. Initiating a live demonstration, layers of steel partitions whirl away to reveal the vivid room of whispers and flavored mouthwashes encouraged by Rasaan to ease client fear. As the manicured professionalism unfolds among displays both vivid loco, Otto crashes the event with his activists wearing inverted scrubs and banners chanting for a “humane dentist.” In the ensuing chaos, Marla and Felix find themselves pinned down to display humanity's fraught role: within health technology against reckless ambition. Events spiral into mayhem, producing meltdowns in robotic protocols impossible to warrant without sacrificing patient interactions, leaving Felix to reckon against harsh facts on rehumanizing dentistry.
