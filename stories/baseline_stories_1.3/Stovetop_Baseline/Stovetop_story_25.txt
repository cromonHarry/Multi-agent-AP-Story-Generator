In the year 2045, culinary technology has advanced far beyond traditional stoves, with “Stovetop” now serving as both a metaphor and a brand name for an entirely augmented cooking experience. Smart houses come equipped with integrated AI-assisted cooking systems designed to manage everything from recipe selection based on nutritional analysis to 3D food printing directly from the pantry. Claire, a rebellious culinary artist disillusioned by this impersonal trend, yearns for the days of cooking as an expressive and hands-on experience. Her neighbor, Sam, is a tech-savvy foodie who champions these advancements as a way to explore dwindling resources effortlessly. Meanwhile, James, Claire's older brother, is often rumpled and distracted, managing a supply chain that keeps the local agriculture alive, and Amelia, a minimalist defensive food critic, questions the morality of substituting human effort for AI efficiency.

Tensions surface at the local Future Foods Expo, where Claire showcases her vision of a sustainability-focused food movement centered on leisure cooking, while her booth is juxtaposed against Sam's spectacle of holographic cooking classes that promote efficiency. As crowds gather around Sam's wonders, Claire has only a few patrons admiring her intricate, handmade meal preparations and the organic ingredients sourced from James. Feeling the societal rift widen between the convenience techno-cooking offers and traditional methods backed by mindfulness, Claire outlines her grand ambition, the Underground Stovetop Collective – an underground kitchen initiative to teach and practice intentional cooking. Unbeknownst to her, Amelia enigmatically observes despite having intentions to provoke her feelings about the ethics of food production. Suspense mounts when ABSolutions, the corporation driving advanced cookers, offers Claire an escape from her struggles by aiming to enlist her expertise in lifestyle venue development—an opportunity fundamentally against her values.
