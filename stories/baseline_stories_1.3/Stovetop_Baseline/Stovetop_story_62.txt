In the year 2045, the remnants of traditional cooking have seen a radical evolution with the advent of intelligent appliances. New stoves are no longer merely simple heat sources, but interconnected hubs of culinary artistry powered by artificial intelligence. Tomica, a passionate culinary artist, runs a small restaurant called SizzleSpot, where she manages half a dozen NeuroStoves—hyper-intelligent cooking appliances that learn from and adapt to their user's styles and preferences. Each stove boasts its own personality, helping to elevate the dining experience beyond the mechanical drudgery of ancient cooking methods. Tomica’s bland culinary competition keeps pressure on her, and as debates about the creative importance of AI rise, Tomica worries if her reliance on technology will cost her the authenticity of her unique style. 

Amidst this culinary turmoil, Aiden, a young food critic, believes in the value of organic cooking—that a truly profound recipe comes from the heart of a chef, not from the circuits of an appliance. Fueled by skepticism of NeuroStoves, he pens harsh critiques that have swooped down on Tomica’s recent experimental dishes crafted with the stoves input. Tomica finds herself at a technological crossroads; she sees potential in their AI but can’t shake Aiden’s harsh words. Compounding her frustrations is Mira, her restaurant's robotic assistant, tasked with keeping the NeuroStoves running optimally.
