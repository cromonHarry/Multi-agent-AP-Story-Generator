In the year 2045, the world was a marvel of technology and culinary arts, but one mundane element remained ever-familiar: cooking. In urban environments dominated by towering megastructures and vast networks of artificial intelligence, chefs still relied heavily on one unassuming piece of equipment—the stovetop. But the evolution of the stovetop was reflective of the societal shifts around it. Embedding AI-driven smart technology into everyday kitchens, households utilized interactive stovetops that could adjust heat and cooking time based on what users wanted to prepare. Four inhabitants under one roof—the aspiring master chef Nadia, her elderly but fiercely knowledgeable grandmother Lila, her tech-savvy brother Omar, and their wise-cracking friend Quest—grappled with both the limitations and potential chaos the new technology presented. With Lila reminiscing about traditional cooking methods, Nadia dreamed of crafting gourmet dishes single-handedly, showing off versatility in her startup culinary channel.

With their kitchen doubling as Nadia's culinary outpost, love for food and nostalgia clashed daily. As Nadia aimed to secure funding from sponsors by pulling dazzling recipes together via the new AI stovetop, she often succumbed to bold yet reckless creations that featured site-specific challenges. Frustration mounted as the stovetop’s AI diagnostics—or “Chef Assist” as they had labeled it—ended up cooking through her recipes far too quickly or inexplicably doing its own bidding and ruining her masterpieces. Family dynamics soon strained under glamorous pretenses as Lila dismissed her modernist attempts, divulging wisdom drawn from years of culinary stewardship, while now-techie Omar argued against the nostalgia in Lila’s “voodoo” cooking.
