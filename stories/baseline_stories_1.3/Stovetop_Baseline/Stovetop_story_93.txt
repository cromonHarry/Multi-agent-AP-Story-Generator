People connect the Stovetop 3000 to an array of personal profiles that recommend meals based on individual health data, preferences, and even emotional states. Olivia, a passionate but struggling chef, feels overshadowed by this technological age, as people would rather rely on algorithm-suggested recipes than trust instinctual home cooking. Joining her in a shared workshop are Lance, an AI programmer, motivated by logic and proficient in machine learning; Rhea, a food scientist seeking to modify natural ingredients for better efficiency in 3D-printed cuisine, and Zane, a food critic, enthusiastic about both tradition and innovation. With their varied motivations and temperaments, Olivia longs to reignite the idea of curated, authentic meal preparation rather than robotic recipe obedience.
