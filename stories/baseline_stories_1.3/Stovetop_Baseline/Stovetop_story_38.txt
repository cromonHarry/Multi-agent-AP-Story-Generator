In the year 2045, humanity has redefined the kitchen, and the once humble stovetop has transformed into a central hub of customizable culinary experiences. The next generation stovetop, known as the "Synthegrill," uses advanced neural networking and augmented reality to enable chefs to create any dish imaginable from the comfort of their kitchen. In this world, four protagonists navigate the evolving landscape of gastronomic technology: Mira, a budding chef striving to embrace creativity while balancing tradition; Airo, her tech-savvy brother developing AI-driven recipe algorithms; Etta, their mentor and culinary purist who condemns reliance on machines, and Lane, a wealthy entrepreneur racing to commercialize the ideas behind Synthegrill for his leading tech company, Elevandon. Unbeknownst to them, undercurrents of competition and philosophical conflict threaten to reshape their relationships and the culinary world itself.

As commercial interest in the Synthegrill skyrockets, Airo’s algorithms garner attention from Elevandon, drawing him away from Mira's more artistic kitchen experiments. His focus directs him toward realism in cooking, both attempting a digital revival of lost recipes and avoiding shortcuts provided by the synthesization process, leaning carelessly into corporate profit at the expense of genuine connection. Flustered by Airo's dedication to commercial fame, Mira begins bringing Etta back into the fold: showcasing the importance of handcrafted cooking, understanding flavors, and bridging the tactical artistry of former methods with modern demands. However, Etta’s strict adherence to the old ways breeds tension, causing Mira to question her alliance. As each character pushes forward in their own vision of what the future of cooking holds, their once-close bonds start to fracture line by line, ingredient by ingredient.

And then chaos erupts.
