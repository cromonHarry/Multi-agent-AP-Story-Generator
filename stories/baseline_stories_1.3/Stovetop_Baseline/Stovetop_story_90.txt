In the year 2045, the culinary landscape has transformed dramatically, fueled by robotics and artificial intelligence. Home kitchens disappeared decades ago, replaced by Community Cooking Domes that serve entire neighborhoods, where optimized recipes are churned out by quantum stovetops. Clara, a youth longing to the simplicity of fragrant meals prepared on a stovetop, visits her grandmother's old locale, a forgotten culinary sculpture studio now overrun with violets and stagnant memories. Meanwhile, Dayton, a brilliant scientist running high-level Genetic Culinary Engineering Department (GCED), introduces revolutionary tech that promises lazy cooking through neuro-simulators but inadvertently strips away the appreciation of the labor and art form of cooking. Two co-workers at GCED, Adam—an idealistic chef—and Zara—a realist influencer reluctantly opposing the tech's escalation— embark on a mission to rediscover grounded and compassionate cooking, while slowly realizing the often-unseen dark side of the community kitchen model.

As word of Clara’s adventurous streak grows, she unwittingly attracts the attention of the Commune’s cooking manager, Alfie—a fed-up architect of the cooking domes who himself lost passion amid the sterile systematic design. Clara organizes an underground revolution in how her neighborhood experiences cooking by gathering eclectic groups around ballads, tales of stove memories, and home cooking secrets ingrained in each diverse community culture. This correspondence sparks fervent hobbies for camaraderie and togetherness over glittery, monochrome trays that now flood the domes. Meanwhile, Adam experiments with prepping fusion meals while Zara live-streams every experiment, promising to curb toxic technology involvement. Tensions rise, leaks of culinary potential disturb GCED’s peace while redefining culinary bases amidst grassroots enhanced dining successes.
