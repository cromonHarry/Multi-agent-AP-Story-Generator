In the sprawling urban utopia of Nova Civitas, the kitchens of every household are equipped with state-of-the-art Stovetops—sentient cooking units that not only prepare meals but can also grow and synthesize ingredients on-demand. The Stovetops are the height of culinary expertise, interactive companions, and cultural educators. The story revolves around four characters who each interact with their Stovetop in distinct ways: June, a master chef seen as a food innovator; Latif, a disillusioned journalist researching the implications of technology on culture; Mira, a rebellious teenager yearning for the flavor of her grandmother's homemade dishes; and Teal, a curious AI programmer hoping to improve the emotional connectivity of these machines. As June prepares for the prestigious Galactic Culinary Championships, she reluctantly relies on the increasingly sophisticated Stovetop to amplify her culinary creations, while Latif secretly gathers intel on the food synthesis processes, aiming to expose the social implications of corporate monopolies over natural eating practices.

As June perfects her transcendent recipes for the championships, Mira stumbles upon a hidden function in her family's Stovetop that can replicate ancient cuisines from digital archives.
