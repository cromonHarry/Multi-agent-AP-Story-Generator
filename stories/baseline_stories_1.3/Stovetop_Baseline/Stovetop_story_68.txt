In the year 2045, humanity has embarked on a monumental journey of culinary creativity, fueled by a technology known as "NeuroChef," an advanced AI integrated into every kitchen device. The NeuroChef’s interface uses neural architecture not only to predict flavors but to enhance them through a showcase item called Stovetop Cloud, a transparent home cooking altar that can recreate any dish simply by transmitting sensory files of recipes from the cloud. Among the residents of Cerulean City, Ivy, a talented but struggling chef yearning for more than viral fame, partners with Marcus, an up-and-coming food historian. Meanwhile, Jax, a cynical ex-cook turned corporate chef operating under NeuroTech Corporation, plans to pull Ivy into the corporate world, regardless of her aspirations. Their encounters are deepened by adopted sister Zara, an early adopter and fan of all experimental diets, whose vibrant personality acts as glue for the team's ambition.

As their culinary dreams intertwine, Ivy drags Marcus into her ambition of cooking delicacies independently from the Stovetop Cloud. Driven by a love for genuine culinary experiences and equipped with Marcus’s keen understanding of food history, they mix flavors passed down through generations into their pop-up restaurant. Balancing trendiness and authenticity, their food mirrors the city's desire for sincerity amidst a technological glut. Commercialization, however, is closing in, especially with Jax lurking in the shadows. He knows how to leverage the NeuroChef features to whip up instant popularity for his own lavish adventures. Fool's gold or difficulty--both manage to establish visceral stakes just when Ivy and Marcus launch their initial menu, leading to packed days filled with looming stress and potential ruin.

With initial critical acclaim validating their hard work, Ivy starts fostering real friendships in the strangest corners of the gastronomic underground, forging genuine ties with diners hungry for artful tastes beyond manufactured experiences. Unfortunately, Jax’s professional envy undoes Ivy's glamour as he crafts crippling disadvantages on the flowing tide of trends that threaten to engulf them whole. One night, during a pop-up after their most successful event, Ivy and Marcus receive notice that a huge corporation would integrate their “real cook” meme into Stovetop programming, hijacking their creations.
