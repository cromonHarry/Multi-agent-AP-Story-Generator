In the year 2045, the world has transformed into a dazzling hub of inventions, but one common kitchen appliance remains at the center of every modern household: the Stovetop. Now dubbed the Simulated Culinary Interface, or SCI, these smart devices go beyond mere cooking; they can analyze dietary restrictions, suggest nutritional enhancements, and even deploy virtual kitchen interactions with celebrity chefs. In this bustling metropolis, Avery Mills, a brilliant engineer passionate about sustainable cooking, works alongside Brad Talbot, a witty AI chef analyst trying to perfect the sense-of-taste parameters in the SCI. Their new project teammates are Lena James, a food scientist who questions the ethics of AI in cooking, and Zoe Carter, a young parent searching for safe, efficient culinary solutions for her family.

As Avery, Brad, Lena, and Zoe prepare for their foremost marketing reveal of SCI 2.0, tensions unfold. Brad’s insistence on prioritizing high-tech culinary personalization clashes with Lena’s ethical considerations regarding artificial ingredients produced with efficiency but without consideration of health implications. Avery, torn between both perspectives and pressured by the timelines set by corporate sponsors, finds herself uncovering a hidden backlash from organizers who worry about traditional recipes being lost to technology's embrace. Yet the team energetically finalizes updates after a sleepless week filled with unexpected code glitches and impromptu tastings, believing overshadowed innovations could pacify culinary purists clamoring for the familiar favorites.
