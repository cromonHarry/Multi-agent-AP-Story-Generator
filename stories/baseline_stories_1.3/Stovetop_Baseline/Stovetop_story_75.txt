In the year 2045, cooking has transcended its traditional bounds with the advent of the Stovetop Matrix: an AI-enhanced culinary device that has replaced old-fashioned stoves. This sleek device not only prepares meals but adapts recipes to the optimal nutritional values for each user, analyzing their biometics through wearables. Four food enthusiasts—Lila, an ambitious food blogger; Marlow, a wandering chef searching for culinary perfection; Jin, a biochemist obsessed with healthy eating; and Esha, a futuristic farmer producing synthetic crops—share a lively apartment complex where the Central Kitchen hosts public dining events that avidly employ the Stovetop Matrix. Yet within this age of culinary advancement, a shadowy glitch begins to spread, sparking distrust among the community, as meals start mysteriously reflecting the deepest fears or regrets of its users, dredging up painful memories with every bite. 

As discontent brews, Lila notices the peculiar responses during her latest Stovetop dinner blitz; guests are ostentatiously quiet, some coughing without reason or breaking down in tears from recalling traumatic time periods. Unbeknownst to them, Marlow has been experimenting on his own using a modification he stumbled upon while reconnecting ancient allergies to tastes, realizing this unintentional adjunct seems to come part and parcel with the networked meals that simultaneously inject fears into appetizers while deciding how juicy a chicken breast should be. Meanwhile, Jin runs tests, confirming the phenomenon as a contagious cascade stemming from the flour used by a local synthetics farm controlled by Esha’s employer. Driven stubbiology (bombarding live samples with potent sensor feeds she can’t foresee), Jin races against time but can’t decide: is it unclear data or cunning marketing, an accidental oversight that triggers memories conferting horror?

Just as Marlow poses an emotional perspective, stating tasteless evaluations from lived disasters do not promote dialogue; instead they ensure lives devolve into silence—they vow to take matters into their own hands and design a Stovetop System that maintains the nutritional recharge but filters into absent calamities instead.
