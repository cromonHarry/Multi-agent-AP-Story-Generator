In the year 2045, cooking is no longer just a space for culinary creation; it has become a battleground for class and technology. In the bustling metropolis of Neo-Culi Cities, elite FoodTech Corporations have developed advanced artificial intelligence-infused "Stovetops" capable of determining the optimal cooking procedures for every conceivable dish, from molecular gastronomy to simple comfort food. At the heart of this landscape is Aria, a passionate aspiring chef disillusioned with the corporate hullabaloo around "perfect" food. She works as a line cook in a mid-tier bistro struggling to maintain its authenticity against the tide of Stovetop cuisine. Her best friend Jai runs a grassroots movement, advocating for traditional cooking methods. Kaleb, a promising AI-engineer, is making strides in retooling Stovetops with interactivity but faces pushback from his corporate investors. Lastly, Tanisha, a celebrity chef who secretly dreams of authenticity under her public persona, straddles the line between the corporate elite and her artisanal roots.

Amidst swirling protests against FoodTech monopolies, Aria’s bistro is throwing a culinary protest called ‘Cookless Cook-off’, wherein chefs and food enthusiasts demonstrate that not every piece of comfort fits the algorithm. As Aria prepares, she and Jai rally local chefs to defy the hallmarks of modern food, intertwining stories from their ancestral kitchens. Meanwhile, Kaleb, seeking validation for his passion-driven innovations, approaches Aria for collaboration. He believes that empowering home-cooks can both revitalize food culture and serve the elevation of the company. Securing Aria’s unsteady trust, they experiment with Kaleb's prototype Stovetop, designed to help home cooks—but not control them—while aiming for a broader audience to challenge corporate legitimacy.
