In the year 2045, Marlow City found itself at a crossroads of culinary technology, where "Stovetope," an intelligent holographic cooking assistant, had reshaped how society engaged with food. Residents integrated their kitchens with appliances that mapped dietary needs, boosted nutrition, and ensured even non-creatives became culinary stars. The invention of the Stovetope was the magic ingredient, rapidly advancing from base AI technology to a supplemental platform that managed everything from autonomous multitasking of meals to virtual dining experiences with digital chefs. For home-schooled caterers Ben and Samantha, navigating this brave new world brought both success and the threat of obsolescence. Meanwhile, the career-oriented chef Hazel—with ambitions as grand as Mount Monitor through this culinary tech shift—framed Sara, a gourmet model who sought to redefine personal quality cuisine using nothing but her artistic presentation.

As competitive freelance kitchens flooded the market, trouble loomed in paradise. Ben stumbled upon a database revealing shady algorithms controlling Stovetope's recipes, favoring certain advertisers and influencers above originality. Recognizing the implications, he confided in Samantha, igniting their cherished passion for cooking, stools at their shared bar counter serving as brainstorming weaponry against a broken digital culinary landscape. Together they devised a plan to confront this brewing monstrosity as they tried to connect it with local growers as well as sabotage the integrity of televised cooking shows that manipulated taste, outcasting authentic cooks like Hazel.
