In the year 2045, climate control technology has engineered a delicate balance in the Earth’s ecosystems, but unpredictable weather phenomena, dubbed “Hyperstorms,” plague cities with violent ranges of environmental extremes. Contemporary architecture embraces multifunctional solutions, one of which is the Umbrella—a state-of-the-art emergency shelter that automatically deploys, providing refuge from both catastrophic storms and air pollution. Marra, a tech whiz and lead engineer at HavenTech, is developing an upgrade to these adaptive infrastructures. She hopes to create an ultra-intelligent scalability module that receives real-time updates from environmental sensors. Unbeknownst to her, the growing energy demands of this feature strain the city’s resources, igniting faction rivalries between eco-activists and energy tycoons, with implications that envelop Marra and her work partner, Jin, an ambitious young coder attuned to the political undercurrents.

The allure of the Umbrella catches the interest of Max, a rogue hacker determined to undermine the corporate giants benefiting from the ongoing climate crisis. He believes that controlling the shelter's functionality could expose HavenTech’s questionable motives while simultaneously protecting low-income neighborhoods from eviction as climate hazards integrate volatile living conditions. Envisioning himself as a revolutionary hero, Max learns to manipulate the system alongside Harmon, his cerebral partner who wrestles with her personal convictions to protect their vision of reform. As tension boils beneath the surface, public awareness grows, protest rallies erupt, and undercurrents of discontent amplify contrasting philosophies around the fate of the remaining communities sheltering beneath the Umbrellas.
