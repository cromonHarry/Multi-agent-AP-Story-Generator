In the year 2048, humanity has become reliant on advanced technologies that promise to solve every environmental woe. One of the most intriguing developments is the Umbrella, an all-encompassing device created by the conglomerate youInCo. Marketed as a personal environmental regulator, the Umbrella can analyze and filter pollution in urban areas while producing clean synthesized nutrients to sustain its user. Driven by uncertainty about increases in pollution and climate instability, dystopian megacities have a legion of Umbrella-users—individuals claiming them as essential for survival. Among them are Mara, an environmental engineer determined to dismantle the nefarious reach of youInCo; Rafe, a tech-abuser who sees the Umbrella as his personal god, providing solace through dizzying, technological consumption; Ludo, an android who aspires to understand human life through observation; and Anya, a radical eco-rebel fighting against corporate entrapment.

The story unfolds as Mara investigates mysterious reports linking the Umbrellas to increased morbidity among urban dwellers. She learns that the devices are unwittingly channeling harmful toxins back to the atmosphere as they attempt to purify, serving instead as polluters themselves. She sources ancient environmental texts predicting the damage bespoke in cutting-edge narratives. She feels overly alone with her madness, though, sharing nature’s concerns only with Ludo, the android who develops a a surprising knack for human empathy under her guidance.
