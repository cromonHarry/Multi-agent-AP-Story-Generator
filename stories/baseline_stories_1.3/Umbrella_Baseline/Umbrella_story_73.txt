In the year 2143, climate change has drastically altered the planet's weather patterns, forcing society to adapt to extreme conditions. Oversaturated hotspots of incessant downpour, blistering sun, and bitter hailstorms have pushed humanity farther underground or to artificial terrains. Innovative technology makes life easier in this burgeoning urban sprawl, with one curious invention emerging—an advanced wearable called the "Umbrella." This semi-autonomous device resembles a fragmented circle, capable of extending protective canopies with high-tension polymer threads encrypted with weather-sensitive AIs. Four diverse characters find themselves entangled in a fateful quest shaped by circumstances beyond their control. Ava, a relentless weather scientist, spends her time researching atmospheric engineering; Orion, a renowned tech developer at ExtendoCorp, devises wearable technologies including the ultimate iteration of the Umbrella; Lila, a journalist peering beneath corporations’ glossy facades, plans to expose corporate manipulation of climate technology; and Milo, a homeless teen with street smarts, scavenges through the bustling underbelly of hyper-urban sprawls.

The pirates of climate technology aren’t satisfied with just reshuffling elements; they want total mastery of pivotal resources. As Vaughn, a corrupted high-ranking political figure, begins co-opting the latest versions of Umbrella technology to shroud the cityscape, he inadvertently prompts more severe natural disasters in those areas privy to higher emissions. Oktavia Weatherfrontseize intends to consolidate corporate interest but crawls toward an unexpected impasse she wasn’t informed of—the Umbrella mechanics were illicitly programming clandestine environmental controls into everyday gadgets. Infected by uncertainty and encumbered by rising tensions ensued from favored meteor bias, the four characters forge an unlikely alliance—together, they establish themselves a cause to address overheated protests advocating open architectures questioning whether sacrificing the populace for prestige can ever hide the blatant truth of fatal sexism driving climate chaos.

In an unstable ad-hoc sanctuary made from idle drones near their densely populated city, their conflicts ebb between uncompromising loyalty to moral pragmatism and personal ambitions. But a game-changing storm fueled by urban negligence swirls dangerously right around the corner—one such disastrous malfunction on the new Umbrella's part during a deceptive day signifies the failed reckoning. Without a proper plan and facing the emotional turmoil resulting from Orion's accidental grounding behind a malfunctioning prototype-less ship only days before they hoped to intercept it through public measures, adrenaline decelerates weighing heavily on residential divides and forgotten ristics more than they previously disclosed.
