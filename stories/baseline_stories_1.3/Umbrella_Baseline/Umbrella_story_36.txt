In the year 2045, climate change has wreaked havoc on the world, driving humanity to adapt in startling ways. Every regional city deploys Bio-Weather Domes, spherical structures that can manipulate microclimates, shielding citizens from brutal storms, melancholic rain that poisons water supplies, or lethal UV rays from a weakening ozone layer. Below these domes, an insurance ethos has emerged—one that guarantees protection against severe weather events. Amidst this landscape lives Jonas, a cynical journalist forced to defend the distasteful work his agency does disseminating fear. He covers the stories of people innovative enough to amplify or rebel against the corporatocracy that runs Bio-Domes, including Margot, an eco-activist, Sol, a charming salesman of skydome insurance packages equipped with "smart umbrellas," and Kai, computer-built AI responsible for maintaining the domes’ weather patterns.

The failure of their Bio-Dome provokes widespread anguish in District Five, plunging citizens into chaos as heavy acid rain ravages their food supply and vehicles alike. While government officials shove propaganda aside, proposing the restoration of the Dome as imminent and faultless, Margot, fueled by her idealism, chases the truth. She discovers derelict satellites blamed for the dome’s shut down.
