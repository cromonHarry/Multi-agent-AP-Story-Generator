In the year 2147, the world has transformed under the pressures of climate change and rampant urbanization. The Umbrella Corporation has evolved from its nefarious beginnings as a biotech conglomerate to become the centerpiece of environmental protection. They create advanced solar-shielding canopies dubbed “UmbraMax,” capable of controlling microclimates in megacities while flattening extremist weather patterns caused by decades of ecological mismanagement. All urban areas are connected through the Umbrella System, a vast, intelligent network that regulates energy independence and agricultural outputs under the iron grip of corporate governance. Yet beneath the Luminous City’s glimmering exterior lie social divides and mysterious spiritual sects, who believe that humanity has strayed too far from nature.

Among the innovative minds of Umbrella Corporation, there is Solana, a bright eco-engineer driven by her passion for genuine change within her community. Working alongside Victor, a devil-may-care hacker with a bitter past, they push against corporate boundaries to uncover areas of malfeasance—the costing and safety concerns tied to some UmbraMax deployments. Their indie missions bring them to Renjin, a cynical journalist whose mistrust in corporate narratives wields sturdy credibility, exemplified by her investigations into covert operations just below the veneer of Utopian luxury. Together, they uncover rumors regarding the shadow faction, “The Roaches,” whose members possess a disturbing blend of ultra-enthusiastic acceptance of nature and considerable technological prowess, visualizing UmbraMax as sacrifices rendered viable due to Earth’s burning need for resources.

As their investigation deepens, Solana's group against all odds discovers that the UmbraMax technology is not merely sustainable but a source control device vastly altering weather patterns to funnel natural disasters towards less profitable regions—namely, rural areas that maintain resistance to corporate alliances.
