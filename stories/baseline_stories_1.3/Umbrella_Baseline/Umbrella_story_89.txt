In the year 2085, Earth is plagued by extreme weather caused by climate change, leading to increased storms, floods, and unpredictable natural disasters. The world has turned to advanced technology to cope, and one prominent innovation is the “Atmos-Flex,” a multi-functional, nanotechnology-enhanced device that serves as an advanced personal umbrella. More than mere precipitation protection, it offers shelter from intense sunlight, harmful pollutants, and even accompanies its user with localized weather simulations for optimum comfort. Four individuals—Shard, a street-savvy weather hacker; Thalia, a climate scientist obsessed with transparency in corporate weather manipulations; Samson, a manager at an enterprising Atmos-Flex manufacturing company; and Cade, a journalist uncovering gaps in capitalist ethics—find their paths entwined unbeknownst to themselves.

The central focus is on Thalia discovering distressing data on atmospheric simulations incubated within the UMBR Corp, which produces Atmos-Flex devices. Through modified versions of these devices surreptitiously implanted with surveillance tech, UMBR Corp has begun decadently manipulating climate data to enhance sales and maintain control over society's essential responses to extreme weather, all while maintaining the appearance of providing safety. Meanwhile, Shard intends to break into a coding module promised to siphon detection methods away from corporate systems so that the average citizen can detect company-sponsored calamities. Meanwhile, Cade catches whiff of these technical secrets, and his pursuit forces unsettling confrontations between him and the superficially bemuled representatives of UMBR Corp.
