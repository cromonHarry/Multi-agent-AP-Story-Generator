In the year 2045, Earth has become a silent battlefield between consumerism and sustainability. Advanced biotechnology has allowed for the perfect material maelstrom: the city of Novo-Poros runs entirely on a self-sustaining system known as ReLIFE, which can repurpose practically any material into new goods. Paul, a sharp-witted engineer dedicated to ReLIFE, dreams of creating a rudimentary unit that any resident can use to recycle their waste instantly catalyzing targeted goods. Sara, his feisty environmental advocate roommate, struggles to push for legislative action toward a circular economy. On the other end is Theo, a wealthy industrialist peddling his latest breakthrough—a projected endless cycle of disposable goods masked under the veil of enhanced ‘upcycling’. Navarro, an anti-establishment hacker often serving a wide circle of radicals, aims to expose Theo's deceptions as superficial and potentially damaging; his connection to wealth and power pulls strings that leave innovation mired in exploitation.
