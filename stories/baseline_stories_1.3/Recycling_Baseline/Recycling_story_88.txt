In the year 2142, Earth has transitioned into a society where recycling is no longer an afterthought but a mainstay of daily life and technological innovation. Centuries of pollution and waste mismanagement have led humanity to implement "Eco-Cities," self-sustaining environments powered entirely by repurposed materials. In Seattle's Eco-Central, citizens respectfully dispose of their items at “meta-spheres,” advanced recycling structures capable of breaking down objects on a molecular level and repurposing them into new commodities and energy sources. The government's newly formed Citizen Roots Agency oversees this highly efficient network but remains tightly surveilled, leading to rising concerns about freedom and individuality.
