In the year 2050, environmental collapse has transformed society into an intricate system of bio-engineered recycling and material reclamation. Waste is a precious commodity as a new generation of “Dirt Dwellers,” engineered humans connected with the Earth’s ecosystem, survives by turning everyday materials back into sustainable resources. Tang, a passionate environmental scientist; Lex, a cynical hacker with a complex past; Sofia, an energetic activist fighting for social justice; and June, a stern but brilliant engineer, navigate this world where physical waste transforms into product potential through a neural network system known as “The Loop.” Society races against its dangerously depleting resources, eying pioneering DNA scrap technologies and plasma recyclers that promise infinite sustainability extraction, but the informational gap and inequity continue to fester.

Frustrated by her company's corrupt practices of hoarding withhares (waste-to-durables network), Tang discovers that SolarLight Corp has begun leveraging covert techniques to undermine grassroots recycling efforts led by Sofia's organization, Eco Genesis. Meanwhile, Lex runs an underground network that guides citizens toward awareness and informs them of how they can prevent misuse of tech for individual gain. Drawn together by their common goal, the four form an alliance to infiltrate SolarLight Corp and expose this exploitation before they fully control public resources and local recycling efforts.
