In the year 2045, Earth has transformed into a planet obsessed with recycling, driven by a global crisis of waste management. This society thrives on innovative processes that convert materials into new products using automated recyclers known as Synthifabriques. These machines not only break down plastics and metals but are also capable of creating products on demand, thanks to advanced molecular synthesis technology. The skyline is lined with vertical gardens made from recycled products while smog has all but been erased. Our four protagonists, Madeline, a veteran Synthifabrique engineer; Rowan, a passionate environmental journalist; Lila, a scavenger with a gift for finding rare materials; and Orion, a shadowy entrepreneur, are all unwittingly drawn into a conspiracy that threatens to upend this utopia.

Madeline has dedicated her life to perfecting the Synthifabrique technology, but she uncovers anomalies that suggest more recycled materials are being generated than actually exist. Meanwhile, Rowan is digging into questionable emissions reports from Orion’s corporate empire, which dominates synthetic products created from discarded electronics. Lila, brilliant at infiltrating waste dumps, discovers unusual markings on industrial waste—signs that hint at dangerous experimental materials being hidden from both the public and regulators. When Madeline comes to Lila in search of the source of the anomaly, they combine forces, fueled by both practical knowledge and Dublin Root lemon tea. As they unravel mysteries beneath piles of salvaged tech, Little Vans appear at waste sites, efficient but menacing in their own dark intrigue.

Convinced recluses are involved in dangerous recycling practices, our team infiltrates Orion’s primary facility under the guise of corporate testers. Just as they manage to recover colluded documents tying the production of Synthifabriques to a constraining black market of outdated materials to produce immediate “synthetic improvements”, they run headfirst into Orion who reveals he is aware of their investigation. Rage boils over as a confrontation reveals how Orion plans to monopolize synthesization by promising green tech while perpetuating systemic client negation on unrecyclable waste. Stakeholders want naive seduction after a leaked project operates them mere pawns enslaved beneath entrepreneurs’ ambitions at terra nonfiction.
