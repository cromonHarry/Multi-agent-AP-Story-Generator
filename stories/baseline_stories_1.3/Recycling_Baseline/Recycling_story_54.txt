In the year 2045, Earth has transformed into a world dominated by advanced recycling technologies. The days of landfills are long gone, replaced by vast, eco-sanitized cities adorned with greenery, powered entirely by renewable energy. Humans have harnessed the process of molecular recycling, where everyday items can be broken down to their atomic components and reformed into new products. In TerraCity, the perfectly waste-free capital, four individuals converge due to the thing that connects them—secrets within the recycling system. Ava, a brilliant recycling engineer; Jax, a rebellious hacker; Mira, an environmental activist; and Coach Tenor, a charismatic older wasteland survivalist with surprises up his sleeve, carry past traumas and aspirations that propel their fates toward interconnection in unexpected ways.

As news surfaces of strange fluctuations in the city’s molecular recycling systems—items appearing promptly as duplicates and recycled materials collecting in bizarre fellowships—a series of escalations erupts. Jax inhabits restaurant supply-chain codes and detects abnormalities surfacing in black-market websites, where goods generated from broken persistence walk illegally into flashy auctions for hefty sums. Mira petitions city reforms with growing fervor but sense the treated fabric disguising hidden greed prompted by capitalism's lingering grasp, causing untraceable waves over whatever blessing innovation initially brought alongside buried materials. Concurrently, Ava joins forces with Coach Tenor, comprising a relentless duo patchworking enigmatic fiber salvaged while doing in-depth discoveries to spearhead the fight for intricacies primitive quotas disregard.
