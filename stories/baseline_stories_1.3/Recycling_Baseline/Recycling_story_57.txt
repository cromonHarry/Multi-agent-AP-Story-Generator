In the year 2045, Earth is a verdant landscape interwoven with technology, a planet molded by the principles of the New Circular Economy. Recycling has transcended the ugly mythology of landfills and waste management. A powerful global government organization known as New Earth Coalition has enabled the creation of fully automated plant-nodes that disassemble and repurpose any product within seconds. In this altered reality, four diverse characters grapple with the newfound societal norms. Iris is a devoted advocate for environmental sustainability studying biotech methods of sustainable material production, Samuel is a high-ranking officer in the Coalition, Amy is an underground activist fighting against the tyranny of conformity in the recycling process, and Raj, Iris's father, runs a resourcefulness workshop for skill-constrained citizens—the first linear economy that ironically thrives to provoke thought outside the recycled shell.

As New Earth Coalition promotes the success stories of mass consumption without guilt or waste, rival ideologies plunge the populace into conflict. Amy, disenfranchised with the rigid educational system that teaches dependence on machine recycling, reveals plans for Revolution-Circle, a grassroots movement designed to return humanity to hands-on nurturing processes rather than insensate machinery. Meanwhile, Iris digs deeper into a covert archives hidden within the Coalition's technological enhancements proliferating suspicions that the automatic manifestations of used items perpetuate consumerism, rather than quenching desires. The balance comes as collateral when Samuel discovers that Directorate simulations manipulating public ideologies are driven by aspirations beyond mere progress, speculating that wealth through control can blind reason in a supposedly enlightened society.

As Revolution-Circle gains momentum, tensions rise. Guided instructions reveal that Amy's guerrilla unit is on a quest for unfinished raw materials utilized to craft non-digital sustainability solutions, encountering increasingly aggressive reprisals from faction drones owned by the New Earth Coalition.
