In the year 2045, Earth has implemented a unique system called Bio-Repurpose, a technology that can break down non-biodegradable materials and transform them into valuable resources by extracting not just physical properties but also reconstructing them at an atomic level. In this world, humanity has made great strides in consciousness about sustainability, yet so has the presence of Global Recycling Agency (GRA)—an institution somewhat jarring against the rise of corporate greed. Sarah, a passionate environmental scientist, dreams of integrating Bio-Repurpose on a wider scale. Her journeys intertwine with Marco, a witty but disillusioned freelance journalist, eager to expose the truth behind corporate claims. They are joined by Chen, a master bio-engineer harboring secrets about a major flaw within the Bio-Repurpose technology that threatens the delicate balance of human progress. Adding tension is Fiona, a tenacious CEO of an eco-energy corporation that profits off private partnerships with GRA.

Sarah's hope for a brighter future begins to tarnish as incidents within her trials accumulate—the materials they recycle vary in effectiveness and some commodities go unprocessed creating pollution pockets across major cities. When Marco persuades her to investigate further, their budding conspiracy forces them deeper into the corporate motives that dwell within GRA. With the organization less focused on the environment and more on capital, they fan out into a realm of suspicious partnerships inching closer to breaking point. At the blurry border between valid research and exploitation lies Chen's struggle; he discovers that the data regarding energy conversion efficiency is ultimately terminal—the technology emits volatile energy spikes that endanger surrounding ecosystems.
