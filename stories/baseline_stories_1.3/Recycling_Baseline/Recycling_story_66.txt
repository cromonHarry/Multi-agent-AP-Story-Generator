In the year 2045, a global crisis regarding waste and environmental degradation led to the birth of Synthesia, a mega-corporation founded with unprecedented technology that enabled complete material transformation of waste back into renewable resources. In this transformed society, scattered urban centers became architecturally modeled in elaborate curves and green solar gardens, filled with mesmerizing depictions of past waste turnings reclaiming energy and materials. Amelia, a passionate recycling specialist at Synthesia, dedicates her life to resurrecting discarded items through the revolutionary process known as Matter Synthesis. Her close friend and mentor, Dr. Jonas Voss, runs InFinity Labs, an underground group advocating for independent, green technologies outside corporate control, often designing sustainable initiatives amid fierce corporate competition. Joined by Jamie, an eclectic artisan leveraging recycled materials into art and missions, and Max, a savvy young programmer finding ways to expose companies breaching recycling ethics, the four friends navigate complex layers of environmental constraints, corporate morality, and little faith in a world overtaken by vacuous consumerism.


However, devastation strikes when key patents leak from Synthesia, exposing the flaws and potential hazards of Matter Synthesis. As Amelia remains torn between loyalty to Synthesia and the news that the recycling process occasionally releases harmful byproducts, she and the others investigate new EcoGenidis - methods to combat waste with natural biotechnology in its raw yet ritually renewable form that require unobtainable equipment accessible primarily to Synthesia elites. Distrust festers internally, and Amelia soon confronts a problematic alignment: one pedestrian product threatened by Synthesia's derisive view of independent practitioners compels her commitment to smoother operations versus the disruptive innovation fight championed by the labs. Jamie's role skips peaks within abstract artwork as new visions reveal subtleties of organic recycling inspired by '#Terraflux', secret pigments that sprung life in design but wore stains of toxicity amid struggling districts.
