In the year 2142, Earth has undergone a staggering transformation, driven largely by the dire consequences of pollution and resource depletion. Recycling, now a fundamental pillar of society, looks vastly different than in the previous century. Mega-cities tower above lush, re-formed greenery, where individuals live harmoniously with nature through biomimetic design and zero waste policies. Mikhail, a visionary recycling engineer known for his passion for extensive resource recovery, obsesses over closing the loop on synthetic materials. Lily, a compassionate environmental activist, leads community-driven initiatives to educate people on the importance of recycling. Meanwhile, Cassian, the mayor with big tech visions, plans to introduce revolutionary technology dubbed "Nano-Recyclers," mini robot swarms designed to disintegrate waste on molecular levels. Catching the media’s eye, Nico, a disillusioned journalist, sees an opportunity to carve a path back into relevant reporting amid a world dominated by tech-fueled narratives.

As the Nano-Recyclers undergo their lavish rollout, Mikhail finds anomalies indicative of unchecked harm, namely environmental chaos that refuses to die down. Unfazed by praise for the project, he suspects that combining humanity's trust with unchecked power could lead to an age of, not sustainability, but exploitation. To hold powerful figures accountable, he approaches Lily. They aim to spread awareness of the repercussions that technology might wreak on the ecosystem. Together, they recruit Cassian's old friend, Diego, a tech assembler hazy on ethics, hoping he might divulge intuition on the project's potentially malicious ramifications. Undercover working on Cassian's campaign control, school is perhaps in session about the cracks in their approaches masquerading as justice. Yet, overwhelmed because they feel underprepared, tension rises as Cassian becomes fixated on group-think broadcasts declaring their operations ethically expedient.
