In the year 2045, the world had evolved significantly in response to the dire straits of climate change and persistent waste management crises. Cities were populated by self-sustaining green vertical towers, and citizens lived in environments deeply integrated with regenerative technology. The concept of recycling had become not just an act but a sophisticated system known as the Circular Nexus, where everything was reborn through complex biochemical breakdown and cellular automata. Four friends lived in this advanced society: Theo, a resource engineer who designed recycling nanobots; Mia, a biotechnologist focused on breaking down plastics using synthetic organisms; Arnold, a historian fascinated with waste movements from the past; and Zoe, an environmental activist dedicated to fighting the last vestiges of corporate misuse of recyclables. Their lives intertwine within this thriving yet tedious mesh of progress and expectation.

Determined to unveil the truth, she gathers her friends to confront the corporation.
