In the year 2045, humanity has achieved remarkable technological advances to combat the crippling waste crisis, leading to a sweeping network of automated recycling centers that can disassemble anything from plastics to biohazardous materials at the atomic level. These recycling plants are powered by AI algorithms that not only streamline the sorting process but also efficiently reconstitute materials into new products. Lina, a young engineer working at the highest tech recycling facility in Newakkor, has developed a consciousness-aware recycler named RY56, equipped with an understanding of ecology and waste management. Meanwhile, Vera, an environmental activist whose work revolves around promoting sustainable practices among citizens, strives to raise public awareness about responsibly discarded waste. They are joined by Max, a political figure with plans to cut down on Newakkor’s waste-manufacturing credits and use the profits to implement housing reforms for the city’s low-income areas, and Akira, Lina’s close friend, who is a brilliant coder disillusioned by corporate corruption chaining technology.

Just then, Vera and Max clash at a local community forum. Max believes in gentle reformation through regulations and positive incentives, whereas Vera believes coded facilities and consciousness-enhanced technology is paramount and seeks a more disruptive method for accountability—the development of a fracture within commercial waste industries.
