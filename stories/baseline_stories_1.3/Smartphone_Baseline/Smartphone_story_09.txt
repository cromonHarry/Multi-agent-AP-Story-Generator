In the year 2045, the evolution of the smartphone has transcended its original purpose, incorporating artificial intelligence and integrated holographic displays into daily life. The small device, now referred to as the Personal Sentient Communicator (PSC), offering advanced multimedia interfacing coupled with neural networking, is embedded within users’ consciousness via nanotechnology. ROMI, a brilliant but reclusive computer programmer, has been at the forefront of this innovation, driven by a vision to enrich human connectivity and extend creative potentials. Alongside him are Mia, an ambitious journalist seeking the truth behind the pervasive influence of the PSC; Sam, ROMI’s childhood friend and now a tech industry mogul slotted to control the next technical wave ushered in by PSC's developments; and Iris, an ethicist who warns against the profound socio-psychological effects of over-dependence on such technology.

As the story unfolds, it becomes evident that the PSC's entrenchment in society brings with it invasive qualities that were unforeseen by its creators. People are increasingly relying on their devices for emotional support and social connections, often preferring holographic interactions over face-to-face conversation. Mia, intrigued by the duality of convenience and danger posed by the PSC, embarks on an investigative journey to expose the lower tier of what the device’s advancements may involve. Gathering her courage, she delves into the rapidly shifting perspectives around technology facilitated by the PSC, leading her to discover troubling data emerging from confidential sales reports—alluding to spikes in mental health issues universally acknowledged in society post-PSC deployment.
