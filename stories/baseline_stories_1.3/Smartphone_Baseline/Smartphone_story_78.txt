In the year 2045, society is intricately woven into the fabric of smartphone technology, now known as the Syntellect. Syntellect devices are nanoimplants that interface directly with users’ neural pathways, enabling efficient communication, enhanced virtual reality, and augmented interactions in every aspect of daily life. People depend on their Syntellects for emotional support through AI companions tailored to their personalities. Among the city's affluent elite, four distinct individuals inhabit this interconnected world: Lira, a top fashion designer navigating a disorienting society teetering on the edge of AI control; Emmett, a loyal technologist struggling with the ethical ramifications of human-device interface; Ren, a teenage hacker poised to unveil corporate secrets, and Dax, a sardonic journalist eager to expose the fragility of human connection in an increasingly digitized landscape.

As trust in proprietary Syntellect technology wavers among citizens, Emmett uncovers unsettling information revealing that the major corporation behind Syntellect is not only harvesting personal data but subtly adjusting users' emotional responses for corporate profit. He, deeply invested in maintaining user well-being, reaches out to his long-time friend Lira for her marketing expertise. Meanwhile, Ren stumbles upon the same revelations while attempting to breach the corporation's firewalls to liberate data for the city’s disenfranchised. Unbeknownst to the group, Dax observes their pursuits and becomes acutely aware of the ethical implications at play. When the four allies collide at a tech conference, their ideas shimmer with potential yet clash with deep-seated fears, a turning point leading them to collaborate in exposing the doings of the corporation.
