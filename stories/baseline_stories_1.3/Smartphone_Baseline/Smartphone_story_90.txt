In 2045, societal reliance on a new iteration of smartphones—dubbed the "Cognitant"—has reached astonishing heights. These devices, seamlessly integrated with augmented reality and brain-based UI, are more than communication tools; they enhance mental functionality and knowledge absorption. Sam, a cynical coder disillusioned with tech, has engineered one of the last truly independent devices, dubbed “TheoThinks.” Meanwhile, Tara, an ambitious journalist, believes that uncovering the dark side of the Cognitant system could demand major changes in civil liberties. Brenner, Tara’s enigmatic tech entrepreneur uncle, funds her project but has dubious ties to Cognitant Sagacity, the company monopolizing augmented interactions, immersing everyone in a potentially oblivious existence connected solely to technology. As these four individuals converge, their destinies intertwine amidst a world on the brink of revelation or ruin.

Tara wins access to the sprawling corporate complex of Cognitant with the promise to publish a risqué exposé detailing the company’s manipulation of public consciousness. She recruits Sam, seeking his coding genius to act as both a safeguard and coder. As they dig deeper, they discover hidden algorithms designed to surveil users and nudge human interactions toward creating docile consumers of Content Encouraged, a diabolical reprogramming of truths wrapped in varying pill-like baits promoting followership and commercial dependency. Tara's passions bloom while Sam’s fear of technology intensifies, leaning heavily into the concern of human obsolescence. DelNavigator, Tara’s childhood friend and passion-driven engineer restrained by experiments on digitized empathy that lack reciprocity in emotion, begins running covert trials off-the-books under Brenner's watchful eye while uprooting family secrets intertwined with Keen Wisdom Team, revealing the monsters His uncle supposedly dabbles with.
