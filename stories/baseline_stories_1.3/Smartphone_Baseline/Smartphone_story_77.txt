In the year 2045, smartphones have transcended their original function to become integral to human consciousness. These "Synaptic Devices," implanted directly into the brain, allow individuals to access information, communicate telepathically, and experience enhanced reality—all beyond what traditional devices provided. Zara, a tech-savvy journalist, uncovers alarming discrepancies in NeuCreate Corp's promotional claims about integration safety after many users experience severe cognitive malfunctions. Frustrated with corporate secrecy and emboldened by her journalistic instincts, she finds herself drawn into an underground group led by Jordan, a brilliant but disillusioned cyborg and ethics critic who advocates for a world disconnected from invasive technology. Also in the group is Malik, a former engineer at NeuCreate who holds crucial insights into the darker side of Synaptic Devices, and Selene, a scientist researching neuropsychic phenomena who believes she can safely extract individuals from compromised integrations.

As Zara’s investigation deepens, she uncovers that rather than enhancing human potential, NeuCreate's products manipulate users' thoughts for profit, using embedded advertising techniques intricately tied to cognitive networks. Consumed by mounting tension, Zara reaches out to Malik and Jordan for support. They decide to crash a high-profile NeuCreate conference, aiming to confront its CEO, Dora Hale, with evidence of their deceptive practices. Editing together a multifaceted presentation capturing users’ testimonies and hard data on malfunctions, they feel a sense of urgency and adrenaline churning within the team. However, the risks become palpable when they discover NeuCreate employs surveillance and security tech far beyond ordinary measures to thwart dissenters.

During the live presentation at the conference, the group's plans descend into chaos when the CEO catches wind of their intimate coding schemes and begins broadcasting their mundane subconscious dystopia, thwarting any dissension before it can surface. In a calculated move, the firm stigmatizes dissent and shifts suspicion back onto the group, branding them as unstable renegades desperate to tarnish their revolutionary product.
