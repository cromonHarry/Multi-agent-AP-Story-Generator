In the not-so-distant future, the concept of the smartphone has metamorphosed into a fully integrated social and cognitive companion known as the Conexus. This single device, implanted during childhood and modular by design, connects individuals seamlessly to reality, augmenting their senses with real-time analytics and insights. Among the users are Mira, a risk-taking journalist ambivalent about her dependency on tech; Jonah, a glitch artist striving to showcase the inherent flaws in a perfect culture; Kim, a tech-skeptical history teacher who fights against the radical transformation of human interaction; and Dex, the brilliant but reclusive designer of the Conexus who struggles with guilt over its consequences on society. As their paths cross, they are forced to confront the personal and communal stakes involved.

Mira lands an assignment that promises to propel her career, pitching an exposé aimed at uncovering the nefarious undertone of Conexus’ omnipresence in daily life. Guided by a latent trust in CRISP—Conexus Radical Initiative Supportive Procedure, a sprawling intelligence force that compels people to practice extreme transparency—she scans through personal data and engagement records of users, initiating her investigation. Kim implores Mira through a borderless educational platform to acknowledge how Conexus undermines authentic dialogue and relationships. When reservation soon turns into crisis, the four of them find themselves convened in a cyber café discussing their divergent viewpoints amid a society increasingly tethered to digital incentives.

As tensions rise at the café, the situation escalates into a heated dispute after Dex reveals that Conexus technology has layers of coding designed to reorder personal experiences based on relationships and commerce rather than sentiments. Their algebra of connections frightens Mira, as she realizes the far-reaching implications—information mishandled can curate America's systemic decay by controlling memories rather than sharing universal bonds. Consequently, the group decides to confront a mass market demonstration for the yet-to-be-released LEVI8—a ground-breaking version that offers interference with consciousness—intending to expose its deviant designs to an audience entranced by glowing screens.
