In the year 2045, smartphones have transformed into integrated neural devices called NeuroLink Pods. These tiny bio-digital implants interface directly with a person’s synapses, allowing thoughts, voice, and even emotions to be broadcast like holographic images projected into shared spaces. Yara, a regulatory oversight officer, spends her days ensuring the creation of artificial neuron pathways keeps up with government guidelines while battling mounting concerns about privacy and mental acuity. Her childhood friend Jamal, a tech prodigy at ComSyn Technologies, develops groundbreaking apps designed to enhance the user experience. Dree, a privacy activist, fights for the rights of individuals, convinced that these implants enable a new era of mind control, distracting society from real-world engagement. Lastly, Felix, a former NeuroLink engineer turned freelancer, has begun distilling ancient knowledge that manually disrupts NeuroLink's algorithms, threatening to unbalance user reliance on their devices.

When two NeuroLink activists—highlighted on a viral infomercial sponsored by Dree’s movement—mysteriously go missing after challenging ComSyn’s latest update of NeuralSync, Yara is drawn into a surreal landscape of conspiracy and high-stakes sabotage. Reports of people having out-of-body experiences as they explore synthetic womb realities fed via NeuroLink intensify her fears of the tech's exploitation. Cue week-end competitions for public perception steer Jamal to submit his error-proof gem of an app amidst claims from Dree’s faction opposing vulnerability.
