In the year 2045, smartphones have evolved into sleek, multi-functional implants known as "SmartOptics" that seamlessly integrate with the nervous system. Entire digital experiences, from holographic messaging to augmented reality interactions, are played out in front of the user's eyes, accessible merely by thought. Within this transformed society revolves Lyra, a user experience designer facing a crisis of imagination as she struggles to innovate in a world overflowing with intelligent tech. Joining her are her loyal friend Jamal, who remains skeptical about relying too heavily on technology, and Juniper, an ambitious journalist eager to exploit the technology's potential to champion governmental transparency. Their lives intertwine when they discover glitches in SmartOptics causing dangerous lapses in reality—for some users, it shifts them into distorted digital realms with unpredictable, perilous outcomes.

As glitches escalate throughout the urban landscape, erratic incidents begin to surface manifesting in outlandish scenarios mediated by users’ mind shifts into their deep-seated fears. Holographic monsters, broken memories, and holographic doubt replace real interactions. The numbers increase, giving rise to chaos in the form of ‘CyberShock’, a state where users lose touch with reality and exhibit aggression towards those around them. Despite the escalating risks, an elite company, Sensatech, works diligently on a deeper integration of human experience and data purposed for surveillance, claiming the need to filter users who are susceptible to CyberShock. While Lyra battles to pitch a preventative feature against these gnawing glitches, Jamal believes in rallying people to reject the systemic control from Sensatech.

Compelled by her loyalty toward fixing operations, Lyra self-imposes a deadline to reverse-engineer the problem of CyberShock. Collaborating closely with Jamal to disseminate illicit insurrectionist movements, a developing underground channel catalyzes expressions in hearts unwilling to forsake personal autonomy.
