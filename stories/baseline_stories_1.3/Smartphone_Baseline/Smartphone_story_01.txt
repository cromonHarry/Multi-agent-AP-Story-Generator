In 2045, seamless integration of humanity and technology has redefined communications, with smartphones evolving into the all-encompassing "SmartMatter" devices. These small, pliable devices adapt their form based on the user's needs—from a phone to a holographic display—or even to transforming into smart fabrics to enhance human capabilities. Our protagonist, Ava, is a brilliant innovation engineer at the leading tech firm, Ethos Dynamics, alongside her best friend Leo, a programming prodigy with vast know-how about SmartMatter's neural interfaces. Meanwhile, they are monitored closely by Mira, the highly ambitious production director who aims to leverage their inventions for fortune and glory—a goal unburdened by ethics. Finally, Diesel, a rebellious hacker with revolutionary ideals, believes that SmartMatter is a guise for corporate oppression and has taken an interest in the duo, trying to sway them to his cause. 

However, all is not particularly harmonious at Ethos Dynamics. A bug in a recent SmartMatter prototype, capable of analyzing emotions and manipulating memories, causes uncontrollable augmented experiences among early demonstrators. Disturbing reports surface across the interconnected urban sectors—a select few perceive nightmares as real, leading them into cascading cycles of mental abuses. As these problems arise, Ava aims to find a remedy, gripping at the last fragments of her visionary concepts while sensing the darkened allegiance Mira represents. Fueled by both memory fragments of what technology should represent and Leo's ability to synthesize complex algorithms, Ava dives deeply into troubleshooting the programming errors buried in the fabric of their imminent disaster.

Fears flicker through the thoughts of hallucinatory narratives as two conflicting ideas emerge: to eliminate inefficiencies and bend the minds of humanity for gain or renounce corruption for liberation.
