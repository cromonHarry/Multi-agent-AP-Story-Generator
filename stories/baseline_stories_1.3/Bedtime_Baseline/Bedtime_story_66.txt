In the year 2045, society has entered a phase where sleep is treated like a commodity, with sleep-tech companies providing high-tech bedding and personalized sleep experiences designed to optimize rest and even deliver vivid, custom dream narratives. Sleep Pods, innovative chambers filled with soothing light and soundscapes, have become an essential part of urban living, where anxieties are eased after an increasingly bustling day. Our protagonists—Ava, an overworked Neural Interface designer; Sam, a quiet but curious botanist fascinated by herbal sleep aids; Martinez, an acerbic dream-recall analyst; and Kira, Chief Inventor at Sysnova Dreams—inadvertently tether their destinies as they navigate this brave new world. They each hold inadequacies buried deep in their pasts, grappling heavily with ineffable regrets. Only at night, when they find solace facing the void behind their eyelids, do the burdens lessen, making “bedtime” a highly cherished, almost sacred parenthesis in their frayed lives.

Concealed tensions brew beneath the saturated façade of consumer tranquility brought forth by sleep-tech. A looming breakthrough in sleep experiences by Sysnova threatens to market a system capable of intrusively bypassing spontaneous sleep barriers and utilize quiet traveling of the mind, threatening the fundamental boundaries of dream-sharing. Avery learns through hushed conversations in polyhex meeting rooms that Sysnova ambition isn't just to ensure buenas noches but offers backdoors for anyone desperate enough to avoid their subconscious pain.
