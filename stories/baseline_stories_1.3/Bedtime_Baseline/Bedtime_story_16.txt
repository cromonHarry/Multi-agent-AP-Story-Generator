In the near future, society has transformed how it views sleep, culminating in the advent of "Bedtime Samsung," a colossal tech conglomerate specializing in personalized sleeping experiences. The central characters include Maris, an overworked data analyst struggling to cope with her demanding job, her younger brother Leo, a free-spirited artist captivated by augmented reality, their aging grandmother, Maeve, who laments the loss of natural rhythms in daily life, and Kai, an ambitious corporate strategist wrestling with his role in shaping the humanized sleep agenda. In this world, audacious silicon implants regulate sleep patterns, tailoring schedules to maximize productivity for every individual. While acceptable by unanimous vote of the Global Sleep Council, this integration raises ethical dilemmas among some factions, particularly her grandmother’s support group, who assert that the rapidly advancing tech is leading humanity away from nature's cycles.

Tensions boil beneath the surface as Maris, tasked with crunching data for the next objective of Bedtime Samsung, begins to realize the variance in people’s satiety. Sleep broadcasts, an immersive audiovisual selling point of the implants, begin to incite family rifts that cripple the household; Leo grows addicted to passion projects embraced by synthetic likeness, disconnecting him from the family and revealing grasping platitudes paraded by his sister. Meanwhile, Maeve often intrudes with stories of past evenings filled with family discussions and laughter, but her fond memories stir guilt in Maris, who increasingly prioritizes deadlines. The sweet young grandmother yearns for connections no longer celebrated in their hyper-demanding lives. Amid blending coexistence, events unravel when authorities unveil a midnight blackout coming soon that could deactivate the implants, rendering those depending on technology to surrender the throbbing absence of real intimacy in favor of staggering silence.
