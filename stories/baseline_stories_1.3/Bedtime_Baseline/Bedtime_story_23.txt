In the year 2145, bedtime has become an elusive concept, transformed by the rise of sophisticated nanotechnology and consciousness-altering pills dubbed "Sleepectra." Society has adapted whereby sleep is optional, with the introduction of countless immersive virtual realities called "Dreamscapes". Families now gather to experience simultaneous Dreamscapes at the stroke of "Future Twelve," designed to promote social bonds while artificially substituting the lost essence of restful sleep. Among these families is young Ava, a curious twelve-year-old wishing for the simple comforts of a bedtime story. Opposed to these developments is Benjamin, her idealistic father who believes in organic rest. He struggles to reclaim simple traditions in a world where engineered experiences overshadow nighttime rituals.

As Ava observes her parents’ growing distance, she grapples with the lonely reality of her hyperconnected world—a life spent in curated dreamscapes devoid of the imagination and warmth found in her late mother’s stories. Benjamin plans to host a “Vintage Bedtime Evening” to reignite emotional connections among his family and to remind them of what they’ve sacrificed. He grapples with financial pressure as he runs a business specializing in nostalgia-inducing Dreamscapes—affording them glimmers of a life now confined to sluggish computerized remnants of perfect memories. Underneath burgeoning childhood eagerness, his alienated partner Jordan, a tech engineer enthralled with hybrid-sleep technologies, battles internal demons about leveling down innovative schedules and tethering their daughter to formulas of traditionality oozed in primitivism.

The night of the Vintage Bedtime Evening arrives amidst hush murmurs throughout the lively house designed for Skylines Crafters—in part influenced by spectacle and Orbit Shell efficiencies—a densely populated metropolis briefly painted in soft lighting. As Benjamin confronts indifference led by merciless gadgetry, Ava makes an impassioned plea to capture unnamed heights of youthful imagination by beginning a tale in bittersweet fragmentation. Inspired, the home becomes a spontaneous bedfellow of hearts needing synonyms for togetherness as Benjamin transitions between absent narratives on major adventures woven home with existential dread and fulfilled displacement.
