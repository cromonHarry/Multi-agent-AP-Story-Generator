In the year 2045, societal norms around sleep and bedtime have undergone a major transformation due to the emergence of advanced nanotechnology and virtual reality. With the introduction of "V-Snooze," a device that stimulates restorative brain patterns without the need for actual sleep, people are now required to attend "night protocols"—a mandatory simulation where they experience curated dream sequences while their bodies temporarily rest. The system aims to combat the disabilities of chronic sleep deprivation prevalent among urban dwellers. Jane, a passionate sleep researcher; Orion, an enigmatic V-Snooze programmer; Mira, a sleep-deprived mother; and Kai, a skeptical journalist critical of the system's ethical implications, form an unlikely coalition to unravel the implications of their manufactured dreams and the palpable role of dreams in human experience. 

As communities grow more dependent on V-Snooze routines, evidence arises suggesting adverse effects manifest in increasingly passive behaviors in society. Jane notices that the dreams engineered by technology siphon off genuine creativity and emotion while fostering a homogenized experience agenda. A deviation in the standard protocols triggers unsettling glitches: personalized nightmares permeate the simulated slumber of citizens, threatening their mental health and sowing confusion within dystopian dream worlds. Haunting visions take shape during Mia’s night protocols as she navigates her demanding life as an executive and mother. Resolved, Jane leads her research team along with Orion into outside experimentation areas to track viral anomalies where personalized nightmares originate. 

Tensions mount when Jane uncovers a clandestine corner of the system revealing these horrific disruptions are intentionally incorporated into V-Snooze—a brainwashing tactic designed to pacify future populations. A discrepancy arises as Kai exposes government plans aimed at further iron-fisting society by establishing dictated “dream programs” based on manipulated corporatino profits. While debates ensue within their group, Mira's suffering arises beyond deduction and becomes post-apocalyptic in nature as the unrelief of anxiety chokes the breaths of her young child Eddie. The climactic confrontation invites chaos as this fractured technological sleep paradigm is tested: insisting that dreams insinuate deeper truth and humanity than data-driven aesthetics pave.
