In 2053, societal norms have evolved to fit seamlessly into a highly connected and automated world. Residents of mega-cities exist under the sanctuary of personalized biomes that provide every comfort, at the cost of natural stimuli and traditional bedtime practices. Children are outfitted with SleepSync devices — neuro-muscular implants adjusting their sleep cycles to optimize performance — traded restful nights for peak productivity. Ellie, a curious teenager, feels increasingly discontent, shackled by the device's powers controlling her and denying her the age-old ritual of natural sleep. Her older brother, Max, works for Virtue Integrated, the bioengineering company behind SleepSync. Their friend Romi, alongside energized buddy Lukas, plays video games in VR environments with colorful avatars symbolizing mythology, merrily navigating quests while pushing deeper into synthetic experiences.

Unbeknownst to them, something more sinister threads through SleepSync’s innocent exterior; recent data hacking incidents suggest deeper than data theft. Worried about their condition, the group conspires to evade surveillance systems and trace the source of these anomalies.
