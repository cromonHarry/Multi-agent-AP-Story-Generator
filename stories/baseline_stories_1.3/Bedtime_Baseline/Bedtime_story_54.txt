In the year 2045, the concept of "Bedtime" has been redefined by advancements in technology and societal transformation. In Core City, a sprawling metropolis where sleep has become a commodity, Ada, a ten-year-old who adores classic bedtime stories, wistfully dreams of a time before virtual temporal beds held sway. Her father, Lysander, a weary but loving dreamcaster at one of the many sleep-accommodation centers, grapples with the age-old question of the cost of progress while struggling to remain connected to his daughter amidst the demands of his job. Her mother, Kaela, a successful detoxification engineer involved in another lucrative sector of the sleepless cycle, tends to view families rather as machines—cogs that contribute to their 24/7 society. Marten, the family's high-tech companion android designed for child interaction, knows more about Ada's heart than any adult. To him, bedtime is a wondrous opportunity for genuine closeness and creativity.

One busy evening, when Lysander returns home to find the emotional distance stark between them, he notices how Ada consistently plays out her stories with Marten but lacks the warmth of simple nightly rituals. Feeling dejected, he recalls the treasure of bedtime stories he cherished as a child; the tales filled with wonder, darkness, and ultimate peace. With Kaela often distracted by work, Lysander seizes the opportunity to create synthesis worksheets that prompt storytelling and encourage wonder in Ada’s daily routine. Struck by the power of storytelling, he offers their old capsule stories in progressive chunks to sleep technicians digging deeper and deeper into minute sleep optimization strategies, slowly igniting conversation among inhabitants on the cultural significance of shared mythologies. 

While Lysander and Ada grow closer through impromptu nightly storytelling, news breaks that the Admin Coalition is about to launch a new phase of sleep tech involving direct convergence of sleep feeds, surging autocomplete functions that render bedtime videos and fables for every age redundant. At a community gathering attended by technology enthusiasts and parents worried about their children's increasingly emotional detachment, Lysander discovers numerous sensibilities bubbling under layers wrought with entrepreneurs’ entangling partnerships structured against the creative, diverging desires of families holding swiftly to a shred of tradition.

An unexpected ruckus breaks out when dread circles among the gathered, sparking differing support for the desire to suspend full convergence in hopes of generating deeper emotional experiences for families with new strict policies around Unit Interaction Labor Hours program implementation. Reflecting this core sector that's valued advertising automation above true expression in society comes to a boil when preparing for community race simulations—a civil architecture resoundingly profits from functionality normas over minds tethered cumbersome relationship imbalances back home instead disconnecting.
