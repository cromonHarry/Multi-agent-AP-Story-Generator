In the year 2058, society has transitioned to a hyper-digital age where humans interact with technology through Nervoso, an advanced neural interface that eliminates the need for physical passwords. This device connects directly to the user's thoughts, seamlessly adapting to their neural signatures. The world is predominantly controlled by IntelliNet, a conglomerate that has monopolized data safety, maintaining that these neural linkages are the ultimate security. Central to this story are four friends: Sam, a tech journalist who revels in the mysteries of adulthood; Tara, a revolutionary hacker advocating for technology enabling through ethical standards; Didan, a skeptical financier wary of IntelliNet’s grip on society; and Neema, an unsuspecting college student disillusioned by the alternative realities presented to her by her aging parents.

As discussions about digital privacy and consciousness intensify, Tara stumbles upon unsettling information suggesting that profoundly sensitive data linked to citizens' thoughts is being exploited by IntelliNet. Concerned about privacy violations, she shares her findings with the group during a BuzzX party, an underground gathering of like-minded innovators. The celebration abruptly turns serious when they discover they are being watched—someone disguised as a western-style sponsor is monitoring their interactions. The tension is palpable as it becomes clear that they are discussing potentially devastating revelations regarding password invasion techniques involving manipulation of casual consumerity. Rallied by an increasing sense of urgency, Tara tricks Sam into actuality by heroically redefining her hacker ethos during their meeting.

Feeling a unique blend of trepidation and determination, the four friends unite to dig further into her evidence.
