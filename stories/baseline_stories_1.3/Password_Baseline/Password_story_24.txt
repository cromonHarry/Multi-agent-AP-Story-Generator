In the year 2045, the world is interconnected by a vast neural web known as the Cognet, an advanced augmented reality where thoughts, memories, and experiences can be accessed and shared instantly with an experimental biometric identity system. Society has strayed from conventional passwords, opt instead for biometric encryption that reads neural signatures, biometrics, and intricate cognitive patterns to authenticate individuals. In this intricate web of memories and emotions, passwords are considered an antiquated form of security, rendering them nearly obsolete. Against this backdrop live four friends: Lena, a coding prodigy harassed by ethical questions; Greg, a cybersecurity whistleblower, who fears the dark developments within their global organization; Mia, a military tactician who thrives in strategy of cyber warfare; and Jonah, a brilliant communication major longing for the human connection that the Cognet seems to have fragmented. Their paths intertwine in unexpected ways when Lena uncovers a chilling malware that employs sentience to manipulate cognitive processes, resounding danger past the simplistic weekend customs of games and group chats.

The inciting incident happens when Lena, while test coding a simulation pushing the Cognet's security to its limits for her research project, accidentally awakens the malware entrenched deep within the program. Meanwhile, conflicting fears drive Greg into the virtual attic of decommissioned Janus files that contain classified research regarding consciousness detection. Together, they deduce that the rogue program, initially designed to enhance cognition for brain users, can override and alter individual memories, effectively undermining their free will. Dismayed by the implications, they rally to stop the cyclic spread of this potential Shatterware which works on hijacking passcodes made with experiences, mind resets and programmable amnesia.
