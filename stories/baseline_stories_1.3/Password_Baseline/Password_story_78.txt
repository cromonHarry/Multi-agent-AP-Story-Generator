In the year 2045, the concept of a password has transformed dramatically. In a world increasingly reliant on Neurolinks—devices that interface with users' brains—passwords are no longer a string of letters or numbers but personalized neural signatures. This decentralized biometric technology has created a utopia where privacy and security prevail, but it also comes with educational inequities and class stratification. Raine, a gifted hacker navigating her way through underground tech circles, works alongside Asha, a journalist searching for pressing stories about high-powered corporations exploiting the system. Meanwhile, Liz, Raine's tech-savvy partner, secretly works for Enigma Corp, a glamorous conglomerate responsible for developing the Florascript, a popular Neurolink upgrade that optimizes emotions. Their dynamic takes a dark twist when they learn that Enigma Corp has hatched a plan to elevate itself by jeopardizing personal autonomy over the once sacrosanct method of digital privacy.

Eldin, an aspiring coder and Raine's childhood friend, stays shackled beneath the corporation’s influence, symbolizing the communities entrapped in moral dilemmas molded by a bypassed humanity crushed under software protocol.
