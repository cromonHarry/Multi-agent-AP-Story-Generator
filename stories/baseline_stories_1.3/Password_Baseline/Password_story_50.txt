In the year 2045, access to information is solely governed by biometrics and mental connectivity, thanks to the invention of the NeuroSync Cube, a device integrated directly into the human brain. Society has abandoned traditional passwords, opting instead for thought patterns and memory-forging algorithms, a system of trust that bypasses physical keys completely. Within this intricate society live Juno, a brilliant but reclusive hacker battling with his past, Clio, an outspoken journalist determined to expose the truth amid swirling tech conspiracies, Rahm, the enigmatic corporate mogul who controls much of technology, and Astrid, Clio's best friend with a secret that intertwines their fates. Clio focuses her efforts on uncovering how NeuroSync has positively and negatively impacted lives, but her deepest urge is to dismantle the barriers between people that these enhancements now erect.

As Clio investigates NeuroSync Corp, she soon discovers shadowy undertones: they are altering thought patterns of citizens through underhanded Royalist protocols that enrich those in power. Meanwhile, Juno skims through the deepest recesses of this corporate web from the underbelly of the city, using his own hacked NeuroSync device to locate anyone who has ever attempted to send an undermining thought toward the data-cores run by Rahm. Patterns trigger invisible alarms disabling accounts and even erasing memories of dissent. He encounters Kastor, an unknown figure among the digital thorns, who thinks like him and helps Juno crack hall monitors watching the cognitive highways of data streams sealing knowledge away. Unbeknownst to both, Rahm is privy to everything and begins to mount trans-dimensional charges against Juno for attempted memory sabotage with relinquished civic trust loopholes as leverage.
