In the year 2045, society has transitioned to a hyper-connected reality, where personal data drives all forms of interaction and livelihood. In this world, a revolutionary biometric identification system known as "Trustkey" has rendered traditional passwords obsolete. Trustkey merges genetic fingerprints, ocular scans, and neural sentiment mapping, allowing individuals instantaneous access to private data, networks, and secure locations—essentially reprogramming the idea of privacy. The story introduces four main characters: Lia, a tech-savvy hacker surviving in the megacity of NeuroTech; Hadrian, an idealistic tech revolutionist advocating for digital freedom; Max, Lia’s estranged father who works for the monopolistic super-corporation Framework; and Ava, an empathy-enhanced AI companion constantly reconstructing human behavioral models, exploring emotions as distinct from mere logic.

As the world edges toward what Hadrian terms an "autocratic digital utopia," fears emerge regarding security and invasions of privacy due to Trustkey's omnipresence. Lia decries the danger of a single access point for one’s entire identity, lambasting the idea that individuals’ emotions might be exploited by a financial system opaque but powerful in its reach. Unbeknownst to her, Max is diligently reporting back to Framework about burgeoning anti-Trustkey entities. When Lia uncovers a flaw in the neural mapping software, she is determined to distribute the exploited code, believing it could destabilize the commercial fabric and encourage sufficient doubt among users. Faced with imminent danger, a bizarre alliance emerges when Hadrian, fearing loss of freedom after attending one of the secret info-meetings, chooses to assist Lia against the corporate machine.
