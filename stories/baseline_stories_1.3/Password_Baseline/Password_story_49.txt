In the year 2045, society has evolved to operate in a realm where traditional passwords have become obsolete. Digital implants replace standard authentication measures in individuals' bodies by connecting their neurological signatures to secure networks. Eyela, a skilled hacker and digital activist, belongs to a group called “The Lumens,” fighting for autonomy over one’s personal data. She is joined by Arin, a brilliant but paranoid programmer who helps ensure their network remains anonymous. Meanwhile, Mira, a twelve-year-old prodigy fascinated by tech, stumbles upon more than she bargained for when she accidentally intercepts part of a wheeling plot orchestrated by a corporation seeking to enhance their hold over society by enforcing bio-passwords. Arsheen, Mira's single mother, instinctively fears for her daughter's involvement with technology and wishes to shield her from the dangers that lurk in the metaverse. 

As The Lumens seek to unite the communities forced into compliance by corporate greed, Eyela discovers a deadly initiative in which passwords have begun melding with essential biological functions. Those willing to cooperate receive artificial enhancements, but at the price of losing their free will. When Mira unravels messages connecting leaders in corporate biotech overwhelmingly embracing the scheme, widened insecurity starts to plague the nation. Toward this uncertainty, Eyela asserts the need to hack the new Ethergate—a seamless communication network connected to rich biocomputers acting as collective identities. Elliot, The Lumens’ designated eyes-brain, warns that the plan may elevate risks, too, calling into question whether victory is achievable without casualty. Nonetheless, Eyela decides to plug herself into the Ethergate’s mainframe, confidently believing authentic changes lie permeated in achieving vulnerability.
