In the year 2045, coffee has transformed far beyond its humble origins. The beverage now exists as a complex bioengineered substance known as “Cafinject,” designed to cater to various psychoemotional states. Instead of simply brewing a cup, inhabitants can now send specific DNA samples to a central lab, which then synthesizes a directly tailored coffee mixture accessible as a sterile capsule. Four characters navigate this brave new caffeine-laden world: Amani, a top Cafinject engineer disillusioned by corporate ethics; Loris, a barista advocating for prebiological coffee's nostalgic charm; Cyril, an underground coffee advocate resurrecting natural coffee beans; and Janie, Amani's data-analyst partner, who resists the debilitating addiction emergent from over-Cafinject use.

With Loris hosting illegal real-coffee events in rebellion against the synthetic wave, Amani becomes embroiled in their efforts pretending to be one of them while trying to find a safer alternative that empowers rather than enslaves individuals.
