In the year 2042, coffee has transcended its historical role as a simple beverage; nanotechnology has revolutionized its production and consumption. In this world, bioengineered coffee plants grow better in harsh climates, and their products contain tailored nutrients and performance-boosting compounds tailored to individual needs, courtesy of an AI named Brewmaster 16. Zephyr, an ethereal yet stressed corporate employee, often finds solace in this enhanced coffee. Her best friend, Amira, a rebellious barista who dreams of a coffee-free world, shuns synthetic beverages and seeks to start her own café serving an ancient, artisan-styled brew. Jung-Soo, a brilliant nanotech engineer, aims to uncover the hazards tucked away in the small print of Brewmaster 16’s design while trying to hide his love for Zephyr. Their lives are intertwined, with each fueled by their own blend of aspiration, critique, or even malice toward a society increasingly mediated by sophisticated algorithms.

As the world moves alongside incessant consumption of customized coffee, strange health effects begin to surface: anxiety spikes, erratic sleep patterns, and lethargy that suppresses creativity within vital digital white-collars like Zephyr.
