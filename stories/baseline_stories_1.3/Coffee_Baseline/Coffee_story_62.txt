In the year 2043, the world thrives in a digital landscape where humans have enhanced their bodies and minds with advanced biotechnology. Coffee, once a simple beverage, has transformed into a sophisticated neural enhancer that not only provides energy but augments cognitive capabilities. Unlike its original form, the new “CaffiSynth” is delivered through neuro-pods embedded in cafés around the globe. These assemblies cost the same as a customary cup of coffee but sculpt individual experience based on the user's mood, biology, and time of day. Lisa, a spirited barista and rebel against complete digital integration, tends to her café in Neo-Tokyo, where Sophie, a wise industry-changer, frequently opts for the CaffiSynth Maxi, their most potent product. There’s also Ray, a mentor-like, tech-savvie barista, and Darko, Lisa’s childhood friend and nagging evangelist for all things tech. Each grapples with their own philosophy concerning the implications of their day-to-day addiction.

As business at Lisa's café subsides, signs of negeri (digital fatigue) take shape across society, leaving patrons dependent on CaffiSynth merely to engage with reality. With massive corporate entities taking hold of the CaffiSynth industry, individual artistry fades. Strange reports surface of users plunging deeper into existential chaos as new adjustments wiped out the essence of both authentic genes and their existential personality. Exasperated, Lisa devises a plan to create a new blend using traditional coffee and unique heritage grains sourced from a small island owned by the Faux Coffee Co., drawing attention to the value of simplicity over complexity. Along with Ray's cautious encouragement and Sophie’s persuasive business knowledge, they seek to find and resurrect renowned pearl roast flavor hidden on the island. However, Darko takes an exposing stance against his friends’ pursuits, asserting that conventional coffee, representing their carefree childhood dreams, holds back progress.
