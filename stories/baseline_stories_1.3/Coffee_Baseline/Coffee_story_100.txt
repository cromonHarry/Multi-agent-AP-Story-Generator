In the year 2043, the world had undergone tremendous change, significantly transforming almost every aspect of daily life, including something as mundane as coffee. From nitrogen-infused elixirs served by robotic baristas to hyper-caffeine pills designed for the on-the-go worker, the global obsession of the caffeine-containing drink had reached an entirely new high. At the heart of downtown chronotropolis was BrewTech, a startup led by four ingenious co-founders: Mia, a passionate bio-engineer; Sean, a technology prodigy specializing in neural interfaces; Ava, a revolutionary in agricultural biotechnology; and Jason, a sustainability enthusiast with a creative flair. While their venture was focused on the next generation of coffee, investigative protocols revealed issues in their proprietary formula, faultlines that endangered both the community and stakeholders alike.

When sales of BrewTech’s neuro-infused caffeine hit an all-time high, Mia imposed additional safety measures that minimized stimulant side effects while maximizing productivity boosts. However, unbeknownst to them, some of the side effects were slipping under the radar due to an accelerated approval process and plummeting ethical standards. As more customers shared stories of erratic behaviors and addiction encounters, cracks began to form within their venture as Ava warned the team about the farming ethics forcing them to use harsher synthetic additives to keep up coffee demand. Sean argued for more efficient machinery to maintain product volume, while Jason clamored for a return to traditional brewing arts and sustainability concepts. Tensions grew as whispers circled of competitors planning to expose BrewTech’s secrets, threatening to ruin foundlings bound by dream.

When the tensions peaked, Sean proposed a high-stakes experiment: creating a Theogenic line of coffee, fusing natural ingredients with AI neural enhancement, feeding user thoughts directly into BrewTech’s systems. Fuelled by caffeine, greed, and desperation, the team pushed the experiment under hyperspeed. The day of its launch culture, people flocked, eager for exploration and adventure like none other civilization had yet experienced. But just when they prepared for consumer output and hype, a logbook showcasing extensive safety mishaps tumbled down amid a nonsensical archetype. Half understandings soared among workers, who endured euphoric highs that metamorphosed into neural chaos.
