In the year 2042, coffee has undergone a monumental transformation. Rather than the ground beans consumed in liquid form, java is now synthesized from genetically modified microorganisms that can produce the caffeine and flavor of coffee on demand. Hector, the chief scientist at Virishane Corp, pushes the boundaries of this technology, believing he can create a superior product that will elevate the market and gain an edge over his competitors. Elara, an enthusiastic barista turned baroness of boutique coffee shops, emerges as a consumer advocate, pushing for authentic coffee experiences amidst the "robotic beans" hysteria. There's also Niko, a food journalist with a penchant for exploring new tastes, who rides the waves of change, documenting the cultural implications of coffee's evolution. Finally, Mira, Hector's childhood friend and a prominent environmentalist, stands firm against synthetic coffee, fearing the impact these organisms have on biodiversity and local farmers.

As the proliferation of synthetic coffee spreads through cafes and retail outlets, Hector's latest development promises a pricy but unbearably euphoric instant version dubbed “Caffeinova.” Meanwhile, Elara bemoans customers who now stay loyal to a point-and-click beverage, AI because of its immediate effects and astounding vibrant flavors. With her latte art completely outdone by advanced mixers, Elara refuses to surrender her identity, rather returning nightly to her cozy shop, where free-spirited baristas design brew rituals from the best Middle-Eastern imports where Alcportae bushes still grow. As Niko delves deeper into these mixtures, mixed reviews accumulate, sparking debates on societal dependency where caffeine becomes an idealistic mirror motivated by science and hedonism.
