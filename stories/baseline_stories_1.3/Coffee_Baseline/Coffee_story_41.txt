In the year 2045, coffee has transcended its traditional form, becoming a pivotal element in society driven by human genius and technological innovation. The global warming crisis, combined with the displacement of natural coffee-growing regions, compelled a group of scientists called the Aroma Coalition to genetically engineer a synthetic coffee bean called ‘Cop26’—labeled both an environmental miracle and potential hedonistic vice. In this bustling world of energy-efficient cafés, people now consume Coffee Variants infused with not only flavor but also personalized boosts—better focus, relaxation, or euphoria. Among them is Lila, a volatile barista who begins questioning the ethically-tainted creation of Cop26 and its addictiveness to people seeking escape from chaos. Unbeknownst to her, two other main players wheel around: Jonah, the charismatic CEO behind the Cop26 brand, dueling with Merven, a rogue activist determined to expose the true impacts of commodified consciousness driven by synthetic substances.

As demand explodes for Cop26 drinks, Lila finds fame as the canvas for a popularizing coffee trade: therapeutic lattes enhanced with personalized mental health patterns coded into them. Yet, her revelation comes after extended hours spent experimenting with her own recipes, discovering the alarming truth about addiction to Cop26 variations post-caffeine fix.
