In the year 2045, the world has transformed into a high-tech landscape where the last Arabica coffee plants are preserved behind glass in fortified vaults. Climate change has rendered natural coffee cultivation nearly impossible; thus, a revolutionary coffee synthesis technology, known as “HyperBrewing,” has been developed. This advancement allows coffee aficionados to replicate nearly any flavor profile or aroma from a concoction of synthetic molecules and bioengineered pathways. Four central figures emerge from this new environment: Carla, a barista and hyper-coffee artisan, whose passion for crafting coffee pushes the boundaries of culinary delight; Anil, a corporate skeptic and ethical scientist who questions the sustainability of synthetics; Maya, a wealthy influencer intent on promoting the future of synthetic coffee; and Jam, the grassroots activist determined to safeguard the last vestiges of natural coffee in defiance of rampant commercialization.
