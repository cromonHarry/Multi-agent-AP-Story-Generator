In the year 2042, the world of beverage innovation has reached unprecedented heights; coffee is no longer simply a ground seed in a cup but a complex biotechnological achievement known as "Cofea Synthetica." Governed by a multinational corporation, NeuvoBrews, Cofea Synthetica enables people to brew personalized cups of coffee in minutes using advanced nano-extraction processes. A rich blend of real coffee essences, flavor profiles, and health-enhancing nutrients can be synthesized at home using an interactive 3D interface. A dimly lit coffee shop, once a hub of social engagement, feels antiquated against the backdrop of biochemical cafés dominated by engineers and marketers. In this new world emerges Florence, a barista turned service technician, Leo, a rebellious experimental coffee-sommelier, Isla, a brilliant geneticist from NeuvoBrews, and Marek, a caffeine-compromised journalist seeking the truth behind the company's murky practices.

As coffee contributes to the declining mental health epidemic, challenges abound around the dependencies associated with Cofea Synthetica. Growing discontent among “unplugged” coffee enthusiasts, like Florence and Leo, underscores their anxieties related to commodification, individuality, and the romantic aura coffee once fostered. One evening, at her quaint coffee shop, Florence discovers illegal beans—unsynthetic coffee!—smuggled through a covert network, intact with nutrient properties crushed by coffee-scorporation propaganda. She teams up with Leo, who expresses his disdain for artificiality through rebellious tasting events, unabashedly showcasing the essence of true coffee culture. Meanwhile, Marek interviews consumers suffering from isolation and addiction, but feels under investigation himself. The three of them connect over their disdain for the corporate machine propping up this distillate concept, vowing to reclaim the ancient craft of coffee brewing.
