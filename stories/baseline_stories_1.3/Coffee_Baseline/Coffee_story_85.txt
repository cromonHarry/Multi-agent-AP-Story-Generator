In the year 2045, coffee has undergone a profound transformation. As traditional agriculture faltered against climate change, scientists developed synthetically grown caffeine optimized for taste and energy efficiency. Brewed with precision-controlled flavor enhancers, coffee became entertainment in itself, an architectural marvel of tastes mixed using algorithms. In the bustling city of New Neo, Max, an ambitious coffee barista and a reluctant sustainability advocate, tends to his ecological café where patented beans and non-animal products reign supreme. Dr. Lira Tran, a geneticist known for her controversial coffee DNA splicing research, frequents his haven, aiming to perfect the traits of premium bean varieties. Alongside them is Taryn, an environmental journalist writing an exposé, and Leon, a sek-tech influencer monetizing the latest life simulation augmented to every coffee homage scattered across the city.

As Max brews his entrepreneurial masterpieces, he overhears Taryn's inquisitive nature as she sparks a discussion with Lira. Frustration crescendos within him about the unethical implications at work, especially the devastating effects synthetic development wreaked on the earth's remaining remote coffee terrains. Thompson-Corp, the conglomerate funding these ventures, dispatches drones carrying coffee-liquid samples—cheaper and tastier, bypassing sustainability altogether. A flashpoint arises as overly programmable AIs designated for flavor collection appear set to strip legacy producers of the ability to retain their crops. As tensions mount at the café, Max sympathizes with the artisan producers, grappling with sold-out acrimony from thirsting masses and suppressive corporate interests.

The excitement converges as a regional coffee festival approaches where the fate of old-growth beans hangs in the balance.
