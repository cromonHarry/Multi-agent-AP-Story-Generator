In the year 2045, soccer—now referred to as “Hyperball”—has transformed into a hyper-competitive sport that combines traditional gameplay with advanced technology. The game is played in huge arenas equipped with holographic interfaces, drone referees, and player suits that enhance agility and stamina. The four main protagonists are Mia, a gifted hyperball player, her teammate Leo, an optimistic strategy thinker; Coach Halden, who once led their team to earlier glory but struggles to grasp newer technology; and Iris, a tech-savvy rival player from one of their greatest competitors. Relationships on and off the field are strained, not only by fierce competition but also by the societal impact of augmented players, which has controversy swirling around topics of ethics and physical integrity.

As the global Hyperball Championship approaches, Mia’s team, the CyberKnights, grapples with internal strife and shifting dynamics. Coach Halden, who relied on classic training techniques, sighs as his team adapts to ever-changing technologies, and feels out of touch with new training tools that make performance-enhancement make or break players. To better reflect cybernetic advancements, the owners introduce a new hybrid player concept—the "Cyborg Athletes," genetically altered humans integrated with machines. Mia’s convictions about the purity of traditional play pitted her against Leo, who excitedly envisions striking partnerships between humanity and machinery. When iris jokingly dismisses the concept during a rivalry match, tensions flare into almost physical confrontations.

The stakes increase dramatically during a pivotal semi-final match against Iris's team, the Neutrinos, where Coach Halden's decision to go 'all-in' on adopting Cyborg techniques culminates their grit against Iris’s crowd-dominating tech. With advanced dribbling augmentations and immersive holographic tactics deployed, teammates question their old instincts against brandless machined-footers. Mid-match, tension peaks when Mia—captured in entranced calculations on how many nano-thrusters are in her trajectory to goal—is forcibly thwarted while controllingively rejecting an upgrade chip offered by tech monitors.
