In the neon-lit city of New Avalon, 2145, a disquiet hum vibrates in the air, a reminder of the Neural Economy's pervasive grip on society. Ira Wren stands at the forefront of a bustling digital marketplace, her heart heavy with memories of personal betrayal as the haunting scars of memory exploitation fuel her passion for cognitive autonomy. Meanwhile, Dr. Tobias Seraph, a cognitive ethicist, debates the implications of 'Mind Guard Legislation' in a symposium, yearning to bridge the gap between innovation and ethics. Simultaneously, Lyra Holt coordinates a meeting of her Neural Collective, weaving connections among marginalized voices, and Kian Mazin, once a corporate insider, now navigates the shadows, plotting his next moves against unscrupulous data firms. Each character is entwined in a struggle that echoes the ethical decay around them, as they push against a tide of invasive surveillance, determined to reclaim what it means to be truly human.

Together, the characters find themselves entwined in a web of conspiracy, pushing them toward a reckoning with the ethical implications of their society's choices.
