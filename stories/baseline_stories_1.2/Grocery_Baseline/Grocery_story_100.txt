In the year 2045, grocery shopping has transitioned into an advanced, multifaceted experience. With the rise of "auto-synth" technology, groceries no longer entail picking through aisles of fresh produce or wrangling with robotic cashiers. Instead, residents of RevoCity interface with augmented reality and drones to "harvest" their shopping list from a digital garden. Here, everything is customizable and grows at your request, reflecting changes in demand in real-time. Sebastian, an ambitious young entrepreneur, leads a startup offering virtual grocery experiences. His sister Mira, a bioengineer, allies with him to advance their holographic fields that make food impression and delivery intuitive. Meanwhile, Ella and Leo, a retired couple living within the city, struggle to adapt to a world where personal interaction is significantly less important.

As Sebastian works on popularizing his virtual grocery platform, a glitch disrupts operations—consumers receive their goods but at astoundingly high prices, leading to panic among customers. Meanwhile, as Mira seeks more primal inspiration for their tech, she uncovers signs that the food-synth protocols may be hiding something sinister; her favorite green-spiced quinoa is linked to a black market transaction involving a syndicate that has hijacked auto-synth technology. Gathering the courage to confront Sebastian, Mira argues that keeping tax break opportunities small won’t solve the growing dissatisfaction in society, and the siblings’ debate escalates into a passionate clash over ethical responsibilities toward their business model.

As Ella and Leo witness the disturbance unfold within their community, they are drawn into the fray, starting a grassroots movement against reliance on automated synth-grocery solutions. They organize forums where people converge to discuss the value of personal choice and wholesome home-cooked meals, reminiscent of Pre-Auto Times. What begins as a dwindling concern evolves into full-blown cameos from community activists holding live demonstrations in virtual public spaces, arguing against the authoritarian grip providers like Sebastian’s start-up hold over families and how unintended consequences are driving a wedge between them. The tension culminates as the syndicate disrupts one of Ella and Leo's virtual meetings with a hostile takeover of the community neuro-network, seeking to silence them and reassert their elite domination over commercial synth-food culture.
