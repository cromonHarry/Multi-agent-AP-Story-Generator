In 2054, the grocery industry has completely transformed with the advent of smart technology and a global scarcity of resources. Physical supermarkets have become relics of the past, replaced instead by advanced holographic shopping systems known as "Grocery Gates." Citizens interact with a tactile AR interface to receive personalized food recommendations delivered directly to their homes by autonomous drones. Amidst this revolution lives Ara, a tech-savvy grocery designer who strives to make food accessibility equitable. Her close friends, Ben, a nostalgic gardener longing for the sway of a farmed crop, Maya, a reformist activist championing food sustainability, and Leo, an eccentric inventor experimenting with synthetic replenishment, make up a kind of family in this new world of endlessly synthetic offerings.

As the city's consumption profiles become more segmented, the underground "Harvest Clubs" emerge—groups composed of renegade gardeners and growers exchanging real produce for goods deemed artificially inferior. When a skeptical Ara stumbles across a secret Harvest Club meeting, she is torn between her admiration for the authenticity of real food and her profession as a synthesist, which hinges on the taste and presentation of manufactured meals. As the Club collaborates on certain planned illegal food-product raises, she gathers inspiration for a proposition involving her Grocery Gate design to incorporate a customizable feature that helps redistribute excess harvested goods.
