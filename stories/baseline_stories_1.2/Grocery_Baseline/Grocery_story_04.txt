In the bustling metropolis of Neo-Cascade, grocery shopping has transcended the simple act of buying food. Instead, it has become a fully immersive experience where virtual reality crushes the confines of physical storefronts. At the heart of this change is Aiva, a tech-savvy grocery architect who has designed the Holofresh system which uses AI and advanced holography to create a lifelike shopping experience in the comfort of one’s home. Ana, an eco-conscious activist, champions the cause of real, sustainable food production, while Len, a retired warehouse grocer, grapples with his nostalgia for the past—where people imposed a genuine connection to the community and food sources. Their friend Max, an experimental programmer, continuously pushes boundaries, using tech to mix joy with organic ingredients—creating virtual gardens that harvest themselves. 

As the city’s dependence on AI-generated food increases, Aiva unveils a new feature: the "Flavor Generator," allowing shoppers to tailor taste combinations never before possible, leading to buzzing excitement and exponential sales figures. In response, Ana warns of the dangers of hyper-modern grocery shopping, fearing that personal tastes will overshadow crop diversity and traditional farming. As she pushes for a return to localized supply chains and organic police initiatives, tensions mount. Meanwhile, Len feels increasingly displaced as his brick-and-mortar groceries steadily shrink before the innovations that seem to bless everyone but him. He enlists the help of Ana to advocate for conscious consumerism as he and weighty tales of the community’s joyful sales slip through eco-friendly windows to make way for seamless transactions powered by unseen databases.

In the relentless clatter of innovation and capitalism, chaos erupts. News reports reveal a serious health crisis: people who excessively rely on the Flavor Generator are developing unusual symptoms once attributed only to allergenic ingredients. A newly formed Disallowed Produce Coalition takes the collective lead of Ana, Len, and Aiva, hurrying to the system’s data core where Max has stimulating intel hidden. But it becomes increasingly clear that the crisis was instigated by a well-honed pirated software, permitting anti-competitors to sully the good name of everything wrought in technotopia. The four friends challenge themselves to get behind the holographic curtain, racing against time to recalibrate the system and derailing their individual ideas about food production in the face of actual human need.

As the rescue mission unfolds under the ground layers of corporate antics, Ana comes up with vegan hacks for natural food that can be produced sustainably outdoors. Legwork—and comical chaos—ensues between Len tearing down barriers in grocery codes where he locked-up rancid items, while Aiva works wonders rectifying feloniously meddled algorithms.
