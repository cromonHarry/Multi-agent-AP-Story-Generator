In the bustling megacity of Neo-Sanctum, the concept of grocery shopping has transformed into a dazzling spectacle of technology and convenience. All food items are synthesized through advanced bioprinting technologies, allowing citizens to control their diets directly from personal app-based interfaces. Whimsical vending machines blanket the perimeters of the city, each capable of serving up personalized microbiome-balanced meals programmed to the nutrition needs of its users. The vibrant subculture of "Meal Designers" arises from creative individuals who curate unique culinary experiences for the wealthier citizens, showcasing intricate 3D-printed foods. Among the main characters is Lyra, a tech-savvy Meal Designer with aspirations to revolutionize the industry, her childhood friend Joey, an enthusiast for organic produce that has vanished in this new age, her sister Mia, whose health has been compromised by the city’s pervasive synthetic diets, and Hawkins, an old-school grocery owner desperate to keep his legacy and traditional ways alive.

Lyra works tirelessly to develop a new food protocol that would bridge the gap between synthetic meals and organic tastes. She believes that introducing sustainable food networks, such as vertical urban farms, can create a wave of change that accommodates both her innovative designs and the ethical demands of the city. Meanwhile, Joey becomes increasingly disillusioned with the grooves of bioprinting, missing the impulse of fresh produce and nostalgia of farmer’s markets from free-range days. Bonding over Lyra's determination, he points out that her expansive plans to revolutionize the Meal Design ethos could backfire if the disasters unfolding around food accessibility continue. As food riots stir among the lower classes who struggle with nutritional exceeding costs and ethical issues arise, Mia’s ailments worsen— a direct consequence of living on bioprinted rations rather than whole foods.
