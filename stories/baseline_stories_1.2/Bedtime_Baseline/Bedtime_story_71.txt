In the year 2045, nighttime has transformed into an experience far removed from what it once meant. Society's relentless pace mandated that sleep, once a time for dreaming, relaxation, and reflection, became scheduled and monitored, dictated by performance-based algorithms. People now settled into their neural visors each evening, signing off on sleep modes that optimized their potential for the following day. Maintaining productivity was an unwavering expectation, with citizens like Mira, a dedicated synthetics engineer, pushing the boundaries of their cognitive abilities. Alongside her was Jase, her easy-going neighboring auto-mechanic; Pep, Mira's all-driven younger brother, intent on becoming a leading scientist; and Nora, an impassioned activist pushing back against the restrictive norms of the sleep algorithm, advocating for the lost joys of a more natural night.

Recent advancements in sleep technologies have created ideological rifts, bifurcating society between those who embrace compliance and those who rebel. Mira accepts the algorithms’ authority, believing them to enhance her creativity and problem-solving skills. Meanwhile, Jase exhibits curiosity about a forgotten relic—a conventional mattress. He harbors dreams of reconnecting with his motley crew through late-night stargazing while Pep operates solo in the hypnotic trance of algorithm-induced sleep enhancers. Their bonds grow tenuous as Pep becomes more consumed by performance ratings, driving wedges between the friends, and prompting Nora to pour fuel on simmering frustrations, gathering followers who wish to break free from mechanized slumber.

Nora strategically invites Mira to a clandestine “real bedtime” gathering in an underground park, mingling both nostalgic relics—a detailed textbook on REM cycles—and descriptions of an alternative form of sleep unratified by algorithms. She hopes Mira's voice could influence others imploring praise for organic, untouched sleep. Conflict brews as Mira feels torn; instinctively, her productivity mindset begs surrender to the profitable path, while her inherent ghost of a dream starts nudging her old self awake. With such considerable stakes volleying between mistrust and awareness, important choices necessitate data, yet emotions beckon an awakening from corporate fixation.

At the panicked climax, Pep overhears a plan devised to legally enforce regulatory compliance around sleep schedules, resulting in citizens critiquing even brief impulses toward personal affection: spontaneous laughter, kissing under moonlight, or contemplating the mysteries of existence beyond algorithms risks eroding efficiency. Their timing culminates into a fateful struggle amidst presumptuous design thoughts overwhelming Mira's resolve at the meeting at dawn’s break. Against the algorithmic scheduler, she pleads her proposal—the "SubDue Protocol," safeguarding an hour a night free from regulation, encouraging interactions and burdens lifted. The attending crowd validates her fervent revelations along with Jase’s stultifying honesty; stoking awakening more than ennui.

The separation stale divides remain, yet factions implore for serene dream-states, some opposers lifting their arboreal desires.
