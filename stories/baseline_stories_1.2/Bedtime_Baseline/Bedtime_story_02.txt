In the year 2145, society has undergone a radical transformation, governing everything from sleep schedules to acceptable bedtime rituals. With the advent of the Nuvaskin device, individuals now have an advanced click-and-sleep technology that threatens to erase the need for traditional rest altogether. Set in a bustling metropolis where distractions await at every turn, Pippa, a young engineer struggling with insomnia, finds herself increasingly disconnected from the osmosis of human interaction. Her older brother Ethan has embraced the Nuvaskin, prioritizing efficiency over sleep, while their eccentric grandmother Margot—who still clings to ceremonial sleeping rituals—cautionarily regards the device as a means of sleep theft. The world revolves around Conductors, who manage public bedtime schedules to optimize productivity, leaving individuals restless and yearning for genuine connection before sleeping hours are artificially assigned.

As the narrative unfolds, Pippa teams up with Max, a rebellious artist determined to uncover the truth behind the architect of Nuvaskin, a powerful tech mogul named Lucien Norling, who harbors his formidable legacy of human pharmacology to promote sleepless human beings capable of peak productivity. Together, they embark on a clandestine journey that reveals a mischievous plan orchestrated by Norling aimed at eradicating natural sleep altogether. As the unraveling conspiracies come to light, Margot’s superstitions entangle the siblings in a search for ancient bedtime tales, so reminiscent of the shared stories of yesteryear – teeming with dreams, fears, and the dimension-shifting magic of a well-deserved sleep. With the influence of live artistry from Max combined with their innocent wisdom, they create enlightenment shows in fringe venues, seeking to inspire a newfound culture surrounding affiliative and genuine sleep.

As escalating tensions arise, they discover more of Norling's campaign, including genetic alternations made to the Bliss frequencies, subtly aiming to diminish the need for sleep as humans stride toward an insistent evolution mechanized for productivity. The climax heightens to a public demonstration orchestrated by the national bedtime influencers when Pippa, Ethan, Margot, and Max, disguised as conductors themselves, reveal how Nuvaskin blocks neurotransmitter contributions critical for the human psyche. Their improvisational performance ignites memories of long-lost shared tranquility during sleep – humans trying to remember the warmth of vulnerability entailment.

Relief brews as hordes of people deny sleep-stealing electrodes across fabrics and actively de-escalate Nuvaskin’s suffocating allure amid reigniting precepts of bedtime bonding.
