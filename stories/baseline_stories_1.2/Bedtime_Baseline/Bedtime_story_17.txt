In a future where sleep and rest have become commodities, Society has embraced an advanced technology called the SomnoSynth, a neural interface that allows individuals to tune their dreaming experiences and optimize their rest patterns. It’s a world dictated by algorithms and sleep quotas, reducing the human experience of bedtime to a regimented activity governed by virtual rewards. Four characters navigate this new normal: Mia, a weary dreamer who craves authentic rest; Lyle, her tech-obsessed brother who believes in the superiority of synthesized dreams; Tessa, Mia's best friend who has fallen ill from sleep deprivation; and Dr. Arlen, the charismatic head of the SomnoSynth Development Corporation, whose dreams of improvement mask darker intentions.

Mia's discontent reaches a breaking point when she discovers a black market for untouched and "pure" human dreams, free from the synthetic alterations of the SomnoSynth. With Tessa languishing in her bed alive yet virtually unreachable, Mia lures Lyle into her scheme under the pretense of enhanced experiences while she secretly aims to sabotage the very system Lyle reveres. Their plan involves infiltrating a high-level SomnoSynth demo, where Dr. Arlen presents the next version of the dream interface he claims “harbors profound awakenings.” As they navigate through layers of security and deception, Mia yearns to find real connection versus engineered euphoria.

As the demo night arrives, tension escalates when Mia's hack into the SomnoSynth mainframe unveils more than she bargained for—flashes of security footage indicating various users’ brain scans being manipulated, exhibited in delayed cycles which stifle creativity in favor of corporate growth. Confronted by Lyle during their diversion to enact the heist, a heated argument stems between siblings reflecting years of differing beliefs about reality and escapism. Stuck in a concealed storeroom, Mia finds herself wrestling with Lyle's inducements—reassurances about the functionality of the increased personal consciousness presented by modern tech, yet her heart yearns for just one night free of varnished dreams.

In an act of desperation, Mia and Lyle sabotage the function, plunging the demo night into chaos. The participants disperse, neural interfaces short-circuiting as an unintended release of true conscious experiences floods the venue. Instantly recognizing each other's real fears and desires without the filtered lenses of tech, Mia reaches out to her brother, forming a shaky yet discovery-laden alliance just as Tessa and other club members stumble upon them in confusion.
