In the year 2045, Earth has transformed under the control of an all-encompassing orthocratic conglomerate known as EcoGenesis. This corporation has revolutionized recycling, giving birth to a protocol touted as Zero Waste Living. Citizens live in dome-like architectures, circumscribed under a society that fetishizes the cradle-to-cradle principles of manufacturing. However, even these advancements have a dark underbelly. Blind faith in EcoGenesis has created a societal divide, where the wealthy hoard resources while the impoverished scavengers sift through robotic waste bins in the vast, grey Recycling Zones to find remnants of forgotten luxuries. Among them are Enzo, an inventive youth leveraging makeshift technology, Lila, a seasoned scavenger and mother, Darnell, an EcoGenesis developer immersed in company protocol; and Zara, a rebellious journalist intent on uncovering the layers of corporate deception.

As EcoGenesis intensifies its scrupulous agenda to orestrict resource use, Darnell wrestles with growing doubts over the ethics of destroying human innovation under draconian recycling quotas. In a chance encounter, he meets Enzo, whose excitement over repurposed technology fosters inspiration in Darnell. Sensing a restless spirit, Enzo introduces Darnell to Lila—a mother who voices outrage over her daughter’s disappearance a year prior in the clutches of EcoGenesis's chain of production. With her defiance, she reveals that the conglomerate secretly extracts human labor out of Recycling Zones, leveraging children to promote their sustaining block manifestations incarnated physiologically into marketed projects. When they team up with Zara, emboldened by free journalism, they become unwittingly embroiled in the bewitching emergency of a counter-operation to destabilize EcoGenesis from within.

Zara boldly publishes an extensive exposé, revealing human trafficking depths behind EcoGenesis, receiving an immediate response as the CEO counters with devastating PR maneuvers. Meanwhile, Lila, desperate to learn the truth behind her daughter’s fate, tracks down whispers riddled within CircuFantasy communication technology, where young laborers share illustrations born out of trauma.
