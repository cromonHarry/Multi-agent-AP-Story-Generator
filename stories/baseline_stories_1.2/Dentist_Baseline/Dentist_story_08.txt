In the year 2042, dental care has transcended mere visits to a clinic into an interactive experience integrated within virtual reality (VR) and artificial intelligence (AI) systems. The Clara Tech Corporation, once a mundane dental prosthetics firm, has evolved to dominate the market with its Dental emulation and Interactive Spatial Therapy (DIST). Patients don headsets and enter customized dental worlds where they participate in procedures ranging from cleaning to dental restoration, all overseen by AI-powered dentists. Riley Huang, a talented but disillusioned VR designer, is disenchanted by how people have lost touch with real human connections. Her brother Max, suffering from acute dental anxiety, finds solace using the DIST program, which is now the most accessible option for patient treatment. Jaedala, a once-revered dentist, now leads Clara Tech's innovations but struggles with her past and ethical dilemmas at the junction of human touch versus technological scapegoating. Lastly, there’s Theo, a street-smart teenager needing braces but preferring to navigate the fractured healthcare system without technology’s help, believing the dentist's chair is going the way of the dodo.

With the recent news of Clara Tech’s ambitions to replace traditional dentistry with fully VR environments gracing every storefront, the lines between convenience and care divide communities. Jaedala is increasingly pushed by board memos insisting she retire conventional practices completely. She invites Riley to co-develop inspection bots, hyper-focused on crafty bounce-back measures when interacting with patients in DIST and fearing the impacts on young candidates, refusing to teach them essential skills like empathy and patience. Meanwhile, Max becomes complacent within his digital comfort zone, nearly neglecting his oral health. When Theo learns of Max’s excessive reliance on DIST, he breaches Max’s veil of detachment and expresses doubt that technology can fulfill human needs beyond chrome and code. The confrontation spurs Max to rethink if humiliation lies in paper-and-clinics or beyond programmed vectors.

Their conflicts intersect dramatically during a Clara Tech marketing event meant to showcase products rolling into preliminary global launch. Midway through the demo, DIST’s virtual servers crash, leaving thousands in disarray as instant cancellations scramble HQ. This casual catastrophe sees Jaedala striving to reconfigure her system even while trapped in interactive abhorrence radiating uncontrolled brushing and texture malfunctions—leaving practice reducers in panic.
