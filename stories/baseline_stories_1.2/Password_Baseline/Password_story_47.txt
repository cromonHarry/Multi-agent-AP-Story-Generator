In the year 2045, the world had become increasingly interconnected, relying on complex neural networks to govern daily life. In this society, a single thought could unlock a home, a bank account, or approval for a transcendent medical procedure. The simple concept of a "password" had evolved into a deep neural signature—unique patterns registered in individual minds. Elena, an elite data security expert, navigated this new reality, carefully designed to protect citizens by making their inner thoughts the keys to personal security. Despite this, the system had vulnerabilities, and conspiracy brewed beneath the polished surface. Among those entangled in the societal web were Rami, an aspiring hacker, Iris, an underground activist concerned about governmental overreach, and Jacob, a mechanic entangled with criminals who sought to exploit the neural lock technology.

Elena's latest project focused on a biometric hack-proofing upgrade for the Neural Authentication System. Buried within the dark nets of the city's underbelly, she stumbled upon whispers of an enigmatic group saboteurs seeking to wrest control of the neural key usage and implant an alternative “trustless” model: a backdoor that would render their minds vulnerable once again, placing hacking tools into the hands of the crafty. Rami, meanwhile, plotted his removals from conformity—frustrated by the chokehold technology had on human freedom. Investigating in secret, he had caught the attention of Iris, who approached him with plans of confrontation against the central authority, hoping to recruit him in their missions of emancipation.
