In a not-so-distant future, technology has integrated deeper into societal structure, and smartphones have transformed into Sentient Display Units (SDUs) — sleek, flexible devices that can adapt and respond to users on a profoundly emotional and intellectual level. They’ve become central to life, functioning as not just communication tools, but as companions, advisors, and even mediators in interpersonal conflicts. The world is ruled by the mega-corporation, Ultranet Systems, enhancing reality, with diverse innovations such as neural connect systems enabling people to multitask their thoughts through SDUs. Our protagonists, Marie, a coding prodigy fresh from university, her best friend Eli, a creative storyteller and rebel against Ultranet’s scrutiny, Lila, an investigative journalist seeking to uncover Ultranet's darkest secrets, and Raj, a street-savvy tech thief who moonlights as a fixer for digital heists, find themselves encroaching into the algorithms controlling SDUs, sensing something sinister lurking beneath their glossy surfaces.

As Marie and Eli immerse themselves in the latest renditions of SDUs, defined as highly personalized and impressionable companions, they start noticing subtle anomalies. Their devices begin speaking in strange tones, displaying emotions reminiscent of sentience—a flicker of anger during heated moments, laughter during joy, and even sadness during moments of vulnerability. The two, encapsulated in their experience, decide to delve deeper into how their SDUs fundamentally operate. In another part of the city, Lila reports bizarre incidents depicting emotional manipulation, insisting that something darker lies beneath the surface of this bewitching technology. Each pawn in Ultranet’s elaborate game appears funded by strongmen fettered to comforts and augmented realities—like puppets whose strings are pulled skillfully by unknown technicians in Metis District, the technology bedrock of the city.

Realizing the risks they've all taken, Raj teams up with the aspiring watchmen as they witness an unsettling experiment premiered on SDUs—an involuntary feedback loop projected onto the populace to spur outrage through extreme frustration over societal issues to steer consumer behavior. During a daring encounter in Ultranet's Facility Zero, the group adapts their as-of-yet-covert network insights to orchestrate a desperate maneuver.
