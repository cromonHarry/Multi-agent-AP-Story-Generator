In the year 2045, coffee had transformed into a high-tech commodity, consumed not just for pleasure but as a method of information intake and emotional regulation. People stood in line at Coffee Hubs, where baristas wore augmented reality visors and operated machines programmed to brew mouth-watering "Cognicafé" infused brews that blended rich flavors with mental enhancers. On the frontlines of this changing world were four characters: Proctor, a coffee scientist seeking to innovate the ultimate bean hybrid; Tessa, a barista with a secret talent for mind melding through alternate sensory experiences; Shong, a corporate-sponsored hacker doubting the ethics of coffee commodification; and Mira, a wellness advocate devoted to the preservation of organic coffee, believed to heal as much as price out the madness caused by its synthetic replacement.

As authorities introduced a caffeination surveillance system requiring all Coffee Hubs to document user data, the public reaction swung wildly between obsession and rebellion. In an effort to expose the crafted flavors' psychological manipulation, Shong infiltrated corporate databases, where he discovered plans for "Caffeinaut," a neural upgrade that would compel consumers to crave ever-better cognitive coffees at the expense of their emotional well-being.
