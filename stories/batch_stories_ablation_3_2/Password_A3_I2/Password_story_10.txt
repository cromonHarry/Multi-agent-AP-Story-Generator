In the year 2147, the world is strained under the weight of the Neuroprivacy Crisis, where emotional transparency has transformed the very essence of identity. Ariella Kahn, a passionate neuroethics activist, fights against the rampant commodification of thoughts after surviving an identity theft incident within the chaotic Thought Society Networks. Parallelly, Dr. Victor Lint, a once-revered neuroscientist, grapples with guilt, haunted by the societal fallout of his inventions. In this society, Sorin Reyes uses cognitive art to challenge norms, while Iris Nyx, driving the elite ThoughtCooperative, capitalizes on the crisis, highlighting deepening divides as emotional currency redefines trust and authenticity in personal connections.

Dr. Victor Lint, witnessing the turmoil, grapples with the consequences of his past innovations, pushing him to secretly aid Ariella in her fight, hinting at deeper moral conflicts ahead.
