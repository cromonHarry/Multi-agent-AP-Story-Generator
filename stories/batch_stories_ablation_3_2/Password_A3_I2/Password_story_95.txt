In the year 2145, the hyper-connected society thrives on a tapestry of digital identities intertwined with emotional resonance, a reality vividly brought to life within the sprawling neural landscape of the city. Nia Solis, a Cultural Sonic Artist, weaves sonic narratives that provoke dialogue around vulnerability and selfhood, often clashing with the prevailing values that prioritize security over personal expression. Meanwhile, Dr. Felix Armitage, a Neuroethics Scholar, battles the normalization of emotive memory theft, urging society to reflect on the importance of personal autonomy in their interconnected experiences. Together, their journeys converge within the heart of the city as they navigate the complex fabric of identity, trust, and the ethical intricacies that define their shared existence.
