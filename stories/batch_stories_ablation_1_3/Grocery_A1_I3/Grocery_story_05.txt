Nearby, Elara Myles tends to her colorful display of freshly harvested produce, ready to share the intricate art behind her vertical farming methods. As the community gathers for the annual 'Eco-Unity Festival,' Maris spearheads a workshop about sustainable grocery practices. Excitement fills the air when a surprise storm rolls in, jeopardizing the festival’s outdoor activities. While busy at her stand, Elara faces difficulties safeguarding her crops, leading to tensions between her idealistic values and the realities of her craft. Zane quickly adapts the immersive grocery space, shifting it indoors while utilizing biofeedback tattoos to gauge attendees' emotional responses. At the same time, Elara grapples with the immediate threat to her vertical farm, showcasing her innovative techniques for crop protection.
