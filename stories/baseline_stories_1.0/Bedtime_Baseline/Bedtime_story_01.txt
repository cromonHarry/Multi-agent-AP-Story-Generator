In the year 2045, the world had fundamentally transformed the concept of "bedtime." Sleep, once considered a vital human function, had become an outdated notion as advanced neural technology allowed for total conscious control over rest cycles. The Sleep Optimization Program (SOP) utilized a neural lace, a thin mesh of nanofibers woven into the brain, synchronizing human circadian rhythms with their personal and professional lives. Emily, a sleep technician, struggled with the ramifications of this technology while caring for her younger brother, Leo, who had grown increasingly dependent on virtual environments. Despite the allure of always being "on," a small, rebellious group known as the Dreamers began advocating for natural sleep, yearning to reclaim the intimacy and mystery of dreaming.

Emily's life takes a turn when she meets Aaron, a charismatic but enigmatic Dreamer who challenges her beliefs about the SOP. He believes that sleep is a sacred ritual, one that humans should embrace rather than manipulate. Curious and intrigued, Emily contemplates the idea of disconnecting from the neural lace for just one night to experience true slumber. Meanwhile, Leo grows increasingly hostile towards the Dreamers, viewing them as a threat to their comfortable, tech-driven lifestyle. Tensions rise when Leo, fueled by frustration, devises a plan to sabotage an upcoming Dreamers' festival, aiming to expose the dangers he perceives in relinquishing control over sleep.

Together, Emily, Aaron, and Leo must decide how to navigate their new reality.
